25.1049.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
Introduced by
Senators Schaible, Lemm, Weber
Representatives Jonas, Pyle
1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact section 15.1-02-11 of the North Dakota Century Code,
2 relating to the accreditation of schools.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
4 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 15.1-02-11 of the North Dakota Century Code is
5 amended and reenacted as follows:
6 15.1-02-11. Superintendent of public instruction - Accreditation of schools - Rules.
7 1. The superintendent of public instruction may:
8 a. May adopt rules governing the accreditation of public and nonpublic schools. Any
9 rule adopted under this section must incorporate measures of student
10 achievement and bear a direct relationship to improving student achievement.
11 b. May provide schools with a choice of accreditation options, including:
12 (1) Accreditation through a state-defined accreditation process established by
13 the superintendent of public instruction; or
14 (2) Accreditation through a nationally recognized accrediting organization, a
15 formal school improvement organization, or a continuous improvement
16 organization approved by the superintendent of public instruction.
17 c. May establish a process to select the accreditation options for schools and a
18 process for schools to select an accreditation option. Development of the process
19 may include input and participation from a steering committee, including
20 representatives of education stakeholder groups, school administrators ,
21 secondary and elementary school principals , and school board members .
Page No. 1 25.1049.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 d. Periodically shall review and evaluate the effectiveness of each accreditation
2 option in improving student achievement and school performance.
3 2. Each accreditation option must:
4 a. Meet or exceed the state standards to ensure consistency in the quality of
5 education.
6 b. Provide measurable outcomes for student achievement, aligning with the state
7 goals.
8 3. A school may select the accreditation option best aligning to the instructional goals
9 and operational needs of the school, subject to approval by the superintendent of
10 public instruction.
11 4. The superintendent of public instruction shall contract with an accreditation
12 organization or vendor to create state-provided accreditation to offer to each public
13 school district at no charge.
14 5. If a district chooses to use an accreditation process from the state-approved list, the
15 district is responsible for any accreditation-related costs.
