25.0931.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
Introduced by
Representatives Toman, Lefor, Weisz, Vigesaa
Senators Hogue, Bekkedahl
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new section to chapter 54-35 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to the creation of a legislative task force on government efficiency; to
3 provide an expiration date; and to declare an emergency.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
5 SECTION 1. A new section to chapter 54-35 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
6 and enacted as follows:
7 Legislative task force on government efficiency - Members - Duties - Report to the
8 legislative management.
9 1. The legislative managemen t shall create a legislative task force on governmen t
10 efficiency.
11 2. Each member of the task force shall serve two - year terms. The membership of the
12 task force consists of:
13 a. The chief information officer;
14 b. The director of the office of management and budget;
15 c. The chief operating officer for the office of the governor;
16 d. One elected statewide officer, appointed by the governor;
17 e. One member of the legislative assembly, appointed by the governor;
18 f. Two members of the house of representatives, appointed by the majority leader
19 of the house of representatives; and
20 g. Two members of the senate, appointed by the majority leader of the senate.
21 3. The member of the legislative assembly appointed by the governor shall serve as
22 chairman of the task force.
Page No. 1 25.0931.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 4. The task force shall :
2 a. Meet at least once each calendar quarter or more frequently at the call of the
3 chairman.
4 b. Review and analyze budgets and reports from executive branch agencies,
5 boards and commissions, and the supreme court .
6 c. Collaborate with and receive testimony from executive branch agencies, boards
7 and commissions, and the supreme court to identify areas to increase efficiency,
8 methods to implement cos t - saving measures, determine areas of state
9 government deemed unnecessary or duplicative, target outdated or overly
10 restrictive regulations, and find areas in which partnerships between the state
11 and private industry would create innovative financing models to drive economic
12 growth and job creation.
13 d. Review the effectiveness and necessity of programs and laws created by the
14 legislative assembly through previous legislative enactments.
15 e. Request the state auditor or an independent private auditing firm to conduct
16 program evaluations using generally accepted performance audit standards for
17 state and local governments.
18 f. Submit an annual report to the legislative management detailing its findings and
19 progress in implementing cos t - saving measures and efficiencies along with any
20 recommended legislation for introduction at the next legislative session.
21 5. A state employee who is a member of the task force is entitled to receive that
22 employee's regular salary and is entitled to reimbursement for mileage and expenses
23 to be paid by the employing agency. A member of the task force who is a member of
24 the legislative assembly is entitled to receive per diem compensation at the rate
25 provided under section 54 - 35 - 10 for each day performing official duties of the task
26 force. The legislative council shall pay the per diem compensation and reimbursement
27 for travel and expenses as provided by law for any member of the task force who is a
28 member of the legislative assembly.
29 6. The legislative council shall provide staffing and administrative services for the task
30 force through July 31, 2031 .

Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 SECTION 2. EXPIRATION DATE. This Act is effective through July 31, 2031, and after that
2 date is ineffective.
3 SECTION 3. EMERGENCY. This Act is declared to be an emergency measure.
