Section 1:
25.1101.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
Introduced by
Representatives Schreiber-Beck, Mitskog
Senator Luick
1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact subsection 5 of section 65-05-07 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to benefits for adaptations to real estate and motor vehicles; and to
3 provide for application.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
5 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Subsection 5 of section 65-05-07 of the North Dakota Century
6 Code is amended and reenacted as follows:
7 5. a. Under this section, the organization may modify real estate and may provide for
8 adaptations and modifications to motor vehicles as follows:
9 a. In
10 (1) Except as provided in paragraph 3, in the case of an injured employee who
11 sustained a catastrophic injury, as defined in chapter 65-05.1, the
12 organization may pay an amount not to exceed seventy-five thousand
13 dollarsone hundred fifty thousand dollars, adjusted annually effective
14 January first of each year after December 31, 2025, by the consumer price
15 index, to provide permanent additions, remodeling, or adaptations to real
16 estate it determines necessary. The dollar limit is for the life of the injured
17 employee, regardless of any subsequent claim. This subdivisionparagraph
18 does not allow the organization to purchase any real estate.
19 b. In
20 (2) Except as provided in paragraph 3, in the case of an injured employee who
21 sustained a catastrophic injury, as defined in chapter 65-05.1, the
22 organization may pay an amount not to exceed one hundred fifty thousand
Page No. 1 25.1101.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 dollarstwo hundred thousand dollars, adjusted annually effective January
2 first of each year after December 31, 2025, by the consumer price index , to
3 provide the most cost-effective, specially equipped motor vehicle or vehicle
4 adaptations the organization determines medically necessary. The
5 organization may establish factors to be used in determining whether a
6 specially equipped motor vehicle or adaptation is necessary. Under this
7 subdivisionparagraph, the organization may not pay for insurance of or
8 maintenance of the motor vehicle. Within the dollar limit and under this
9 subdivisionparagraph, the organization may pay for vehicle or adaptation
10 replacement purchases. The dollar limit is for the life of the injured
11 employee, regardless of any subsequent claim.
12 c. (3) In the case of an injured employee who has not sustained a catastrophic
13 injury, as defined in chapter 65-05.1, the organization may provide the
14 benefits under subdivisions a and b paragraphs 1 and 2 if the organization
15 determines the benefits would be cost-effective and appropriate because of
16 exceptional circumstances as determined by the organization.
17 b. For purposes of this subsection, "consumer price index" means the percentage
18 change in the consumer price index for all urban consumers in the midwest
19 region as determined by the United States department of labor, bureau of labor
20 statistics, for the most recent year ending December thirty-first. The organization
21 shall calculate the annual adjustments, rounded to the nearest five hundred
22 dollars, as soon as practicable at the end of each calendar year and apply the
23 adjusted amount retroactively to January first of the year.
24 SECTION 2. APPLICATION. This Act applies to qualifying expenditures made on or after
25 the effective date of this Act.
