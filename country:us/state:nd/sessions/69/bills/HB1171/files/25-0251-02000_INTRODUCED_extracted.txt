Section 1:
25.0251.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
Introduced by
Representatives Heinert, Dockter, Hauck, Meier, Novak, Porter, Ista, Conmy
Senators Cleary, Cory, Dever
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new section to chapter 15-10 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to the creation of the law enforcement family scholarship program; and
3 to provide an appropriation.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
5 SECTION 1. A new section to chapter 15-10 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
6 and enacted as follows:
7 Law enforcement family scholarship program - Administration.
8 1. As used in this section:
9 a. "Correctional officer" means an individual who has completed a correctional
10 officer course approved by the department of corrections and rehabilitation and is
11 employed by a correctional facility as defined in section 12 - 44.1 - 01 .
12 b. "Eligible student" means an individual:
13 (1) For whom a law enforcement officer claimed a dependency exemption on
14 the law enforcement officer's tax return for the previous year; and
15 (2) Who is enrolled at an institution of higher education under the control of the
16 state board of higher education.
17 c. "Emergency services provider" means an individual employed ful l - time as
18 emergency medical services personnel as defined under section 23 - 27 - 02 .
19 d. "Firefighter" means an individual employed as a firefighter by a political
20 subdivision. The term does not include a firefighter employee of the national
21 guard.
22 e. "Law enforcement officer" means a correctional officer, emergency services
23 provider, firefighter, or peace officer, as defined in this section, who has served
24 ful l - time in that position in the state for at least three years .
Page No. 1 25.0251.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 f. "Peace officer" means a public servant authorized by law or a government
2 agency or branch to enforce the law and to conduct or engage in investigations of
3 violations of the law.
4 2. The state board of higher education shall:
5 a. Administer the law enforcement family scholarship program and adopt
6 procedures necessary to carry out this section.
7 b. Grant a scholarship to an eligible student who applies for a scholarship.
8 3. The amount of the scholarship is a sum equal to fifty percent of the student's tuition,
9 less any other grants and scholarships the student will receive.
10 SECTION 2. APPROPRIATION - BOARD OF HIGHER EDUCATION - LAW
11 ENFORCEMENT FAMILY SCHOLARSHIP PROGRAM. There is appropriated out of any
12 moneys in the general fund in the state treasury, not otherwise appropriated, the sum of
13 $5,000,000, or so much of the sum as may be necessary, to the board of higher education, for
14 the purpose of distributing scholarships to students who are eligible to receive a law
15 enforcement family scholarship, for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30,
16 2027.
