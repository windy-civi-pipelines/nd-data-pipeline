13 SECTION 17. STATE EMPLOYEE COMPENSATION ADJUSTMENTS - GUIDELINES.
14 1. The 2025-27 biennium compensation adjustments for permanent state employees are
15 to average 3 percent per eligible employee for the first fiscal year of the biennium and
16 are to average 3 percent per eligible employee for the second year of the biennium.
17 The increases for the first year of the biennium are to be given beginning with the
18 month of July 2025, to be paid in August 2025, and for the second year of the
19 biennium are to be given beginning with the month of July 2026, to be paid in
20 August 2026. Increases for eligible state employees are to be based on documented
21 performance and are not to be the same percentage increase for each employee.
22 2. The office of management and budget shall develop guidelines for use by state
23 agencies for providing compensation adjustments for classified state employees. The
24 guidelines must follow the compensation philosophy statement under section
25 54-44.3-01.2.
26 3. Probationary employees are not entitled to the increases. However, at the discretion of
27 the appointing authority, probationary employees may be given all or a portion of the
28 increases effective in July, paid in August, or upon completion of probation. Employees
29 whose overall documented performance level does not meet standards are not eligible
30 for any salary increase.
