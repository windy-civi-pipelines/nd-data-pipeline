16 SECTION 15. STATE EMPLOYEE COMPENSATION ADJUSTMENTS - GUIDELINES.
17 1. The 2025-27 biennium compensation adjustments for permanent state employees are
18 to average 3 percent per eligible employee for the first fiscal year of the biennium and
19 are to average 3 percent per eligible employee for the second year of the biennium.
20 The increases for the first year of the biennium are to be given beginning with the
21 month of July 2025, to be paid in August 2025, and for the second year of the
22 biennium are to be given beginning with the month of July 2026, to be paid in
23 August 2026. Increases for eligible state employees are to be based on documented
24 performance and are not to be the same percentage increase for each employee.
25 2. The office of management and budget shall develop guidelines for use by state
26 agencies for providing compensation adjustments for classified state employees. The
27 guidelines must follow the compensation philosophy statement under section
28 54-44.3-01.2.
29 3. Probationary employees are not entitled to the increases. However, at the discretion of
30 the appointing authority, probationary employees may be given all or a portion of the
31 increases effective in July, paid in August, or upon completion of probation. Employees

1 whose overall documented performance level does not meet standards are not eligible
2 for any salary increase.

4 TRANSFERS - REPORTS - APPLICATION.
5 1. Notwithstanding section 54-16-04, the office of management and budget shall transfer
6 funds from an executive branch state agency's new and vacant FTE pool line item to
7 the agency's salaries and wages line item or other line items with salaries and wages
8 funding as requested by the agency in accordance with provisions of this section.
9 2. An executive branch state agency may request a transfer to provide funding for the
10 salaries and wages necessary for the remainder of the 2025-27 biennium for a new
11 full-time equivalent position authorized by the sixty-ninth legislative assembly from the
12 date of hiring through the end of the biennium, limited to the amount identified for the
13 position in the statement of purpose of amendment.
14 3. After July 31, 2026, an executive branch state agency may request a transfer if the
15 agency projects actual salaries and wages expenditures will exceed the agency's
16 available salaries and wages funding for the biennium. The transfer amount may not
17 exceed the amount by which the agency's actual salaries and wages savings from
18 vacant positions and employee turnover to date and estimates for the remainder of the
19 biennium adjusted for other identified uses of any savings are less than the vacant
20 position savings estimate used by the sixty-ninth legislative assembly in development
21 of the agency's appropriation. The agency shall provide documentation supporting the
22 need for the transfer to the office of management and budget and legislative council in
23 advance of the transfer request.
24 4. Each executive branch state agency with a new and vacant FTE pool line item shall
25 report to the office of management and budget and the legislative council on a
26 quarterly basis regarding any transfer of appropriation authority for filling a new
27 full-time equivalent position. The report must include the funding transferred, the title
28 of the position filled, the salary funding removed by the sixty-ninth legislative assembly
29 for the position by funding source as identified in the statement of purpose of
30 amendment, and the date the position was filled.

24 SECTION 17. A new section to chapter 54-44 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
25 and enacted as follows:
26 State facility maintenance fund.
27 There is created in the state treasury the state facility maintenance fund. The fund consists
28 of moneys allocated to the fund from legislative transfers. Subject to legislative appropriation,
29 moneys in the fund may be used for maintenance needs at state-owned facilities, excluding
30 facilities under the control of the state board of higher education.
