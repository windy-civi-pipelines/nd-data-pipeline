Section 1:
25.8078.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
with Senate Amendments
Introduced by
Human Services Committee
(At the request of the Department of Health and Human Services)
1 A BILL for an Act to provide for a legislative management study regarding child care provider
2 licensing; to provide for a program evaluation regarding child care services; and to create a
3 child care services licensing advisory committee.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
5 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE MANAGEMENT STUDY - CHILD CARE PROVIDER
6 LICENSING.
7 1. During the 2025-26 interim, the legislative management shall study the laws,
8 administrative rules, and policies relating to child care provider licensing.
9 2. If the legislative council conducts a program evaluation of the department of health
10 and human services child care services, the study must be informed by the program
11 evaluation.
12 3. The study must include input from:
13 a. The department of health and human services;
14 b. The child care services advisory committee; and
15 c. Child care providers.
16 4. The legislative management shall report its findings and recommendations, together
17 with any legislation necessary to implement the recommendations, to the seventieth
18 legislative assembly.
19 SECTION 2. LEGISLATIVE MANAGEMENT PROGRAM EVALUATION - DEPARTMENT
20 OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES - CHILD CARE SERVICES.
21 1. During the 2025-26 interim, the legislative council shall consider conducting a program
22 evaluation of the department of health and human services child care services.
Page No. 1 25.8078.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 2. The program evaluation must include:
2 a. Review and analysis of state and federal laws, administrative rules, and policies
3 relating to child care.
4 b. Evaluation of the child care services framework, identifying strengths, challenges,
5 and opportunities, including opportunities to streamline the administrative
6 process, to enhance clarity for providers, and to provide services for more
7 children.
8 c. Consideration of licensing requirements, the federal and state assistance
9 programs available, education requirements for program operators, training and
10 certification requirements for staff members, the number of children in an early
11 childhood program, child to provider ratios, and square footage requirements.
12 d. Review and analysis of child care funding sources, expenditures, assistance
13 penetration rates, and the distribution of resources to determine whether the
14 program efficiently and effectively uses and distributes resources to support
15 access to affordable and safe child care.
16 e. Review of recently enacted legislation regarding child care services to determine
17 if the policies have been timely implemented and properly administered, including
18 an analysis of measurable goals, performance data, and options to improve
19 enrollment.
20 3. By August 1, 2026, the legislative council shall report its findings and
21 recommendations to the legislative management.
22 4. The legislative management shall report its findings and recommendations, together
23 with any legislation necessary to implement the recommendations, to the seventieth
24 legislative assembly.
25 SECTION 3. CHILD CARE SERVICES ADVISORY COMMITTEE - DEPARTMENT OF
26 HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES.
27 1. During the 2025-26 interim, the department of health and human services shall
28 establish a child care services advisory committee to review and provide input on the
29 laws, administrative rules, and the department of health and human services policies
30 relating to child care licensing.
Page No. 2 25.8078.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 2. The department of health and human services shall provide staffing and administrative
2 services for the child care services advisory committee.
3 3. The child care services advisory committee shall collaborate with the department of
4 health and human services to conduct a comprehensive revision of administrative
5 rules relating to child care licensing. In coordination with the committee, the
6 department shall facilitate a series of regional meetings and topical focus groups with
7 child care providers from around the state to discuss child care licensing challenges
8 and review proposed revisions to the administrative rules. The committee must have
9 the opportunity to review and provide input on all of the department's proposed
10 administrative rule changes and policy changes relating to child care.
11 4. The advisory committee shall consist of:
12 a. Two child care center representatives, appointed by the legislative management;
13 b. Two group child care representatives, appointed by the legislative management;
14 c. Two preschool program representatives, appointed by the legislative
15 management;
16 d. Two members of the house of representatives, selected by the majority leader of
17 the house of representatives;
18 e. Two members of the senate, selected by the majority leader of the senate; and
19 f. A child care expert, appointed by the legislative management.
20 5. The child care services advisory committee shall provide periodic status updates in
21 collaboration with the department of human services on proposed licensing rules and
22 a final report to the legislative management by June 30, 2026.
23 6. The child care services advisory committee shall dissolve after providing the final
24 report to the legislative management.
