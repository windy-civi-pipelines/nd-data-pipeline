16 1. If a report of a conviction of a traffic offense, or admission or adjudication of a traffic

27 under section 39-06-33. For the purposes of this chapter, the director also may receive
28 and act on reports of traffic offense convictions forwarded by federal, military, and
29 tribal courts in this state.

1 40-05-06. City fines and penalties limited.
2 1. Except as provided in subsectionssubsection 2 and 3, the fine or penalty for the
3 violation of any ordinance, resolution, or regulation of a city may not exceed one
4 thousand five hundred dollars, and the imprisonment may not exceed thirty days for
5 one offense.
6 2. For every violation of a city ordinance that regulates the operation or equipment of a
7 motor vehicle or which regulates traffic, except those ordinances listed in section
8 39-06.1-05, a fee may be established, by ordinance, which may exceed, by up to one
9 hundred percent, the limit, for an equivalent category of violation, set forth in section
10 39-06.1-06.
11 3. For every violation of a city ordinance enforcing the requirements of title 40, Code of
12 Federal Regulations, section 403 relating to publicly owned treatment works, or
13 prohibiting shoplifting, vandalism, criminal mischief, or malicious mischief, the penalty
14 may not exceed a fine of one thousand five hundred dollars, imprisonment for thirty
15 days, or both such fine and imprisonment.
16 4.3. This section does not prohibit the use of the sentencing alternatives, other than a fine
17 or imprisonment, provided by section 12.1-32-02 for the violation of a city ordinance,
18 nor does this section limit the use of deferred or suspended sentences under
19 subsections 3 and 4 of section 12.1-32-02.
20 SECTION 5. AMENDMENT. Section 40-11-12 of the North Dakota Century Code is
21 amended and reenacted as follows:
22 40-11-12. Commitment of guilty person for nonpayment of fines or costs.
23 Any person upon whom any fine or costs, or both, has been imposed for violation of a
24 municipal ordinance may, after hearing, be committed upon order of the court to jail or other
25 place provided by the municipality for the incarceration of offenders until the fine or costs, or
26 both, are fully paid or discharged by labor as provided in section 40-18-12. The court may not
27 commit a person under this section when the sole reason for the person's nonpayment of fines
28 or costs, or both, is the person's indigency. An order of commitment under this section shall not
29 be for a period in excess of thirty days. As used in this section, "fine" does not include a fee
30 established pursuant to subsection 2 of section 40-05-06.

22 40-11-12. Commitment of guilty person for nonpayment of fines or costs.
23 Any person upon whom any fine or costs, or both, has been imposed for violation of a
24 municipal ordinance may, after hearing, be committed upon order of the court to jail or other
25 place provided by the municipality for the incarceration of offenders until the fine or costs, or
