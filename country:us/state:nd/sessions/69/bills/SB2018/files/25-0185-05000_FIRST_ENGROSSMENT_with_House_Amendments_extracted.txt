18 SECTION 14. OTHER FUNDS - STRATEGIC INVESTMENT AND IMPROVEMENTS
19 FUND - UNCREWED AERIAL VEHICLE REPLACEMENT PROGRAM - LEGISLATIVE
20 MANAGEMENT REPORT. The other funds line item in section 1 of this Act includes the sum of
21 $16,000,000 which the department of commerce shall use for the purpose of administering an
22 uncrewed aerial vehicle replacement program, for the period beginning with the effective date of
23 this Act, and ending June 30, 2027.
24 1. The department of commerce shall collaborate with the uncrewed aircraft systems test
25 site established in section 54-60-28 and state agencies, including institutions under
26 the control of the state board of higher education, to replace uncrewed aerial vehicles
27 owned by the state which do not comply with requirements of the federal National
28 Defense Authorization Act and the American Security Drone Act of 2023. The
29 information technology department, in consultation with the department of commerce
30 and the uncrewed aircraft systems test site, may implement an enterprise uncrewed
31 aerial vehicle data management solution. An enterprise uncrewed aerial vehicle data

1 management solution implemented under this section must ensure all data collected,
2 transmitted, stored, and consumed by an uncrewed aerial vehicle remain under the
3 control of the state. As used in this section "enterprise uncrewed aerial vehicle data
4 management solution" means a system for secure, end-to-end data collection,
5 transportation, storage, processing, and consumption through enterprise software. A
6 state agency owning an uncrewed aerial vehicle before January 1, 2025, is eligible to
7 receive a new uncrewed aerial vehicle that is in compliance with federal requirements
8 and is of equivalent capability to the agency's existing uncrewed aerial vehicle. The
9 department of commerce shall purchase each uncrewed aerial vehicle based on the
10 needs of the agency and a recommendation from the uncrewed aircraft systems test
11 site and provide the uncrewed aerial vehicle to the agency. The department of
12 commerce shall use a portion of the funding provided in this section to pay for training
13 needed for agency staff to operate the new uncrewed aerial vehicle. The department
14 of commerce and uncrewed aircraft systems test site shall give priority to state
15 agencies willing to share uncrewed aerial vehicles rather than purchasing multiple
16 uncrewed aerial vehicles.
17 2. Any uncrewed aerial vehicle replaced under this section must be remitted to the
18 uncrewed aircraft systems test site for proper decommissioning and disposal pursuant
19 to federal and state regulations, which may include the sale of uncrewed aerial
20 vehicles to the United States department of homeland security at market rates for use
21 in the counter unmanned aircraft system program. The department of commerce shall
22 remit any funding received under this section to the state treasurer for deposit in the
23 general fund.
