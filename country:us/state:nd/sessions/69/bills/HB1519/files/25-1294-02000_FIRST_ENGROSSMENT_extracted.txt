Section 1:
25.1294.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
Introduced by
Representatives Rohr, K. Anderson, Frelich, Holle, McLeod, Toman
Senators Clemens, Paulson, Schaible, Van Oosting, Weston
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new section to chapter 23-02.1 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to death and vaccination records.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
4 SECTION 1. A new section to chapter 23-02.1 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
5 and enacted as follows:
6 Death and vaccination records.
7 1. The department of health and human services shall compile and maintain individual
8 death and vaccination records for each vaccine type for which there are records in the
9 North Dakota immunization information system.
10 2. For each individual appearing in the electronic death registration system and the North
11 Dakota immunization information system, the department shall record the following
12 information: unique identifying number, sex, race, date of birth, date of death, vaccine
13 manufacturer, vaccine type, date of vaccination, dose number, lot number, vaccine
14 provider, and vaccination location.
15 3. Within thirty days of the end of each calendar quarter, the department of health and
16 human services shall publish on its website a time-series summary report based on
17 the individual death and vaccination records for each vaccine type that has been
18 administered to more than twenty-thousand individuals in this state over the last
19 quarter. The summary report must :
20 a. Cover all qualifying vaccines administered in this state in the last two years.
21 b. Include the following index columns:
22 (1) Month and year of administration;

1 (2) Sex;
2 (3) Birth year;
3 (4) Vaccine manufacturer ;
4 (5) Vaccine type; and
5 (6) Dose number.
6 c. Include the following value columns:
7 (1) Total number of vaccines administered for the indexed cohort; and
8 (2) Total number of deaths for the indexed cohort occurring within one, three,
9 seven, fourteen, twenty-one, and thirty days of the exact vaccination date
10 and continuing with columns reporting the total number of deaths for
11 additional thirty-day intervals through seven hundred twenty days since
12 vaccination, with the total deaths counted for each interval being cumulative
13 up to that period.
14 4. A redaction may not be made to a report under subsection 3 unless the department o f
15 health and human services is expressly notified of a privacy issue regarding the
16 metrics counted under subsection 3 .
