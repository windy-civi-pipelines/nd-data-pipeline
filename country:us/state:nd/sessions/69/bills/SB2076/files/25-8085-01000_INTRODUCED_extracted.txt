1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact section 50-24.6-04 of the North Dakota Century Code,
2 relating to prior authorization.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
4 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 50-24.6-04 of the North Dakota Century Code is
5 amended and reenacted as follows:
6 50-24.6-04. Prior authorization program.
7 1. The department shall develop and implement a prior authorization program that meets
8 the requirements of 42 U.S.C. 1396r-8(d) to determine coverage of drug products
9 when a medical assistance recipient's health care provider prescribes a drug that is
10 identified as requiring prior authorization. Authorization must be granted for provision
11 of the drug if:
12 a. The drug not requiring prior authorization has not been effective, or with
13 reasonable certainty is not expected to be effective, in treating the recipient's
14 condition;
15 b. The drug not requiring prior authorization causes or is reasonably expected to
16 cause adverse or harmful reactions to the health of the recipient; or
17 c. The drug is prescribed for a medically accepted use supported by a compendium
18 or by approved product labeling unless there is a therapeutically equivalent drug
19 that is available without prior authorization. The department shall work with the
20 medical assistance recipient's health care provider to assure treatment can be
21 found for diagnoses with no compendia supported medications.
22 2. For any drug placed on the prior authorization program, the department shall provide
23 medical and clinical criteria, cost information, and utilization data to the drug use
24 review board for review and consideration. The board may consider department data

1 and information from other sources to make a decision about placement of the drug on
2 prior authorization.
3 3. a. For individuals eighteen years of age and older, exceptExcept for quantity limits
4 that may be no less than the pharmaceutical manufacturer's package insert,
5 brand name drugs with a generic equivalent drug for which the cost to the state
6 postrebate is less than the brand name drugs, generic drugs with a brand name
7 equivalent drug for which the cost to the state postrebate is less than the generic
8 drug, or medications that are considered line extension drugs, the department
9 may not prior authorize substantially all drugs in the following medication classes:
10 (1) Antipsychotics;
11 (2) Antidepressants;
12 (3) Anticonvulsants;
13 (4) Antiretrovirals, for the treatment of human immunodeficiency virus;
14 (5) Antineoplastic agents; and
15 (6) Immunosuppressants, for prophylaxis of organ transplant rejection.
16 b. For individuals under eighteen years of age, except for quantity limits that may be
17 no less than the pharmaceutical manufacturer's package insert, brand name
18 drugs with a generic equivalent drug for which the cost to the state postrebate is
19 less than the brand name drugs, generic drugs with a brand name equivalent
20 drug for which the cost to the state postrebate is less than the generic drug, or
21 medications that are considered line extension drugs, the department may not
22 prior authorize substantially all drugs in the following medication classes:
23 (1) Antipsychotics;
24 (2) Antidepressants;
25 (3) Anticonvulsants;
26 (4) Antiretrovirals, for the treatment of human immunodeficiency virus;
27 (5) Antineoplastic agents; and
28 (6) Immunosuppressants, for prophylaxis of organ transplant rejection.
29 c. The restrictions of subdivision b do not apply for individuals under eighteen years
30 of age, who have five or more concurrent prescriptions for psychotropic
31 medications.

Sixty-ninth

1 d. Prior authorization for individuals under eighteen years of age is required for five
2 or more concurrent prescriptions for antipsychotics, antidepressants,
3 anticonvulsants, benzodiazepines, mood stabilizers, sedative, hypnotics, or
4 medications used for the treatment of attention deficit hyperactivity disorder. The
5 department shall grant authorization to exceed the limits after a prescriber
6 requesting authorization consults with a board certified child and adolescent
7 psychiatrist approved by the department.
8 e. The restrictions of this subsection do not apply if prior authorization is required by
9 the centers for Medicare and Medicaid services.
10 f.c. The restrictions of this subsection do not apply to a medication class in
11 subdivision a if a manufacturer of a drug in that class excludes the departmen t
12 from supplemental rebate offers or value-based purchasing agreement offers due
13 to the existence of the prior authorization exclusion in subdivision a.
14 d. As used in this subsection, "line extension drug" means a new formulation of a
15 drug. The term does not include an abuse-deterrent formulation of a drug.
16 g.e. As used in this subsection, "substantially all" means that all drugs and unique
17 dosage forms in the medication classes outlined in paragraphs 1 through 6 of
18 subdivisionssubdivision a and b are expected to be covered without prior
19 authorization, with the following exceptionsexcept:
20 (1) Multisource brands of the identical molecular structure;
21 (2) Extended release products when the immediate-release product is included;
22 (3) Products that have the same active ingredient or moiety; and
23 (4) Dosage forms that do not provide a unique route of administration.
24 4. The department may use contractors to collect and analyze the documentation
25 required under this section and to facilitate the prior authorization program.
26 5. The department shall consult with the board in the course of adopting rules to
27 implement the prior authorization program. The rules must:
28 a. Establish policies and procedures necessary to implement the prior authorization
29 program.
30 b. Develop a process that allows prescribers to furnish documentation required to
31 obtain approval for a drug without interfering with patient care activities.
Page No. 3 25.8085.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 c. Allow the board to establish panels of physicians and pharmacists which provide
2 expert guidance and recommendations to the board in considering specific drugs
3 or therapeutic classes of drugs to be included in the prior authorization program.
4 6. The department may negotiate additional rebates from drug manufacturers to
5 supplement the rebates required by federal law governing the medical assistance
6 program. Additionally, the department may join a multistate supplemental drug rebate
7 pool, and if the department negotiates additional rebates outside this pool, any other
8 manufacturer must be allowed to match those rebates.
