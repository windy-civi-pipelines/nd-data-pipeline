21 SECTION 3. Chapter 40-18.1 of the North Dakota Century Code is created and enacted as
22 follows:
23 40 - 18.1 - 01. Establishment of a municipal court .
24 1. The governing body of a city may, by ordinance or resolution, establish a municipal
25 court which is a part of the unified judicial system of this state. The supreme court may
26 supervise and sanction municipal courts and municipal court judges. A staff attorney
27 with the state court administrator's office may advise a municipal court judge and clerk
28 on an administrative and procedural court issue.
29 2. The governing body shall, by ordinance, provide for the election of at least one
30 ful l - time or par t - time judge .
31 3. A city that establishes a municipal court shall provide:

2 necessary duties; and
3 b. Necessary supplies and technology and sufficient clerical and nonjudicial support
4 personnel to assist the municipal judge.
5 4. Municipal court must be held at the time and place designated by the municipal judge.
6 The court schedule must be posted publicly by filing the notice with the city auditor's
7 office and posting the notice on the city's website if the city has a website.
8 5. Two or more cities, by resolution, may enter an agreement establishing a single joint
9 municipal court. A copy of the agreement must be filed with the state court

19 the agreement. If the agreement does not include a provision for termination, the
20 agreement may be terminated by any party upon the provision of not fewer than thirty
21 days notice to the governing bodies.

17 1. If reason to doubt a defendant's fitness to proceed exists, as defined under section
18 12.1 - 04 - 04, the case must be :
19 a. Transferred to the district court for evaluation; or
20 b. Dismissed on motion by the prosecutor.

23 remanded to the municipal court for further proceedings.

25 suspended pending determination of the defendant's fitness to proceed.
26 4. If the defendant is found to lack fitness to proceed, the case must be dismissed.
27 5. In proceedings under this section, the city shall provide a prosecuting attorney and, in
28 the case of an indigent defendant, a defense attorney. The city may contract with the
29 county, state, or any person for the prosecution or defense services.

1 40 - 18.1 - 04. Criminal responsibility .
2 1. A case in which the defendant's criminal responsibility at the time of the crime is in
3 question may be transferred to the district court for evaluation upon written request by
4 the defendant. The defendant shall initiate the request within twenty - eight days after
5 arraignment and the district court shall order the defendant to undergo a criminal
6 responsibility evaluation under chapter 12.1 - 04.1 .
7 2. Following the evaluation, if the defendant asserts the defense of lack of criminal
8 responsibility, the case must remain in district court for trial. If the defendant does not
9 assert the defense of lack of criminal responsibility, the district court shall remand the
10 case to the municipal court for further proceedings.
11 3. In proceedings under this section, the city shall provide a prosecuting attorney and, in
12 the case of an indigent defendant, a defense attorney. The city may contract with the
13 county, state, or any person for the prosecution or defense services.
14 40 - 18.1 - 05. Election of municipal judge - Qualifications .
15 1. A municipal judge must be elected to serve a term of four years and may not hold any
16 other office in the city in which the municipal judge serves as a judge. The city auditor
17 shall notify the state court administrator of the election or appointment of a municipal
18 judge or alternate municipal judge.
19 2. Except when prohibited by home rule charter or ordinance, the municipal judge may
20 be a part-time judge and may serve as a municipal judge in more than one city.
21 3. A municipal judge in a city with a population of five thousand or more must be licensed
22 to practice law in this state and must be a resident of the city unless the city, by
23 ordinance or resolution, provides the municipal judge is not required to be a resident of
24 the city.
25 4. The municipal judge in a city with a population of fewer than five thousand is not
26 required to be licensed to practice law in this state and is not required to be a resident
27 of the city.
28 5. The governing body of the city shall fix the compensation of the municipal judge.
29 40 - 18.1 - 06. Demand for change of judge .
30 A party to a proceeding pending in a municipal court may obtain a change of judge under
31 section 29 - 15 - 21. The municipal judge must be replaced in accordance with section 40 - 18.1 - 07 .

3 1. If a vacancy exists in the office of municipal judge by death, resignation, or otherwise,

13 1. The governing body of a city with a municipal court may provide for the office of clerk
14 of the municipal court, which may include deputy clerks of municipal court. The
15 governing body of the city shall appoint the clerk and deputy clerk, with the consent of
16 the municipal judge. The governing body shall fix the salary of the municipal clerk and
17 deputy clerk.
18 2. The clerk and deputy clerk of the municipal court shall issue all process of the court,
19 administer oaths, file and preserve all papers, docket cases, set trials, and perform
20 other acts necessary to carry out the duties and responsibilities of the court. The clerk
21 shall receive, account for, and pay on a monthly basis to the city general fund all fines
22 and forfeited bonds paid into the court.
23 40 - 18.1 - 09. City prosecutor .
24 1. A prosecutor licensed to practice law in this state must be present for all contested
25 hearings and proceedings involving a class B misdemeanor in a municipal court .
26 2. The city shall pay the compensation of the prosecutor and any necessary expenses
27 incurred in prosecuting a violation of a city ordinance.

3 40 - 18.1 - 18. Transfer to district court - Expenses of prosecution - Division of funds
4 and expenses among city, county, and state.
5 1. A defendant may request in writing to transfer the case to district court and to exercise
6 the defendant's right to a jury trial within twenty - eight days after arraignment. If the

9 2. If the defendant waives a jury trial after a transfer to district court, the district court
10 shall remand the matter to the municipal court for disposition and sentencing if the
11 parties agree to a remand.
12 3. Unless remanded to the municipal court by agreement of the parties, the district court
13 retains jurisdiction for sentencing and enforcement.
14 4. The city shall provide a prosecuting attorney and, in the case of an indigent defendant,
15 a defense attorney. The city may contract with the county, state, or any person for the
16 prosecution or defense services.
17 5. The city, county, and state may agree to a division of any fees, fines, costs, forfeitures,
18 and any other monetary consideration collected from cases transferred under this
19 section, which must be paid to the city general fund and the county treasury and the
20 state general fund at least once each quarter. At the time of payment, the clerk of
21 district court shall account under oath to the city auditor, county treasurer, and state
22 treasurer for all money collected. In the contract, the city, county, and state may agree
23 to a division of expenses, including jury and witness expenses, related to cases
24 transferred under this section. In the absence of a contract, all fees, fines, costs,
25 forfeitures, and any other monetary consideration collected from transferred cases
26 must be deposited in the state general fund.

20 3. The district court may affirm, reverse, or amend any appeal order or judgment and
21 may direct the proper order or judgment be entered or direct a new trial or further

23 40 - 18.1 - 21. Municipal judge may enforce orders and judgments and punish for
24 contempt.
25 A municipal judge may enforce compliance with the court's orders and judgments. The
26 judge may fine or imprison for contempt committed in the judge's presence while holding court,
27 as well as for contempt of process issued, and of orders and judgments made by the judge. If
28 an act or omission constituting a contempt in a municipal court is not committed in the presence
29 of the municipal judge, an affidavit alleging the facts may be filed and a warrant of arrest may be
30 issued on which the individual accused may be arrested and brought before the municipal judge
31 immediately. The individual must be given a reasonable opportunity to employ counsel and

2 judge may discharge the individual or adjudge the individual guilty and may punish by fine,

5 40 - 18.1 - 22. Judgment for fines, fees, or costs - Procedure .
6 1. If the judgment imposes a fine or assesses a fee, the municipal judge may order a
7 certified copy of the judgment be filed in the office of the clerk of the district court of
8 any county in the state. The clerk of district court shall treat the municipal court
9 judgment in the same manner as a civil judgment of any district court of any county of
10 the state.
11 2. At the time of filing the judgment with the office of the clerk of district court, the
12 municipal court judge shall order an affidavit providing the name and last-known
13 mailing address of the defendant and otherwise complying with section 28 - 20 - 15 be
14 filed.
15 3. Upon the filing of the judgment and affidavit, the clerk of municipal court shall mail
16 notice of the filing of the municipal judgment to the defendant at the defendant's last-
17 known address and file proof of mailing with the district court. The notice must include
18 the name and mailing address of the municipal court.
19 4. An execution of other process for enforcement of a municipal court judgment filed
20 under this section may not be issued until ten days after the date the judgment is filed.
21 5. If the defendant shows the district court of any county that an appeal from the
22 judgment is pending or will be taken, the court shall stay enforcement of the municipal
23 court judgment until the appeal is concluded or the time for appeal expires.
24 6. The municipal judge shall order the defendant to pay a filing fee of ten dollars to the
25 clerk of district court.
26 7. Upon filing of the judgment with the district court in accordance with this chapter, the
27 judgment is enforceable by the city only in the same manner as provided for a
28 judgment for money in a civil action.

5 40 - 18.1 - 24. Abolition of municipal court in a city with a population of fewer than five

7 1. The governing body of a city with a population of fewer than five thousand having a

9 municipal court abolished by resolution must be transferred to the district court of the

11 2. The city auditor shall provide a copy of the resolution abolishing the court to the
12 presiding judge and clerk of court of the district court for the district in which the city is

14 court administrator. The resolution must provide the effective date of the transfer of
15 cases, which must be at least ninety days following the date of the resolution.
16 3. At least ten days before the effective date of the transfer, the clerk of the municipal
17 court shall deliver to the clerk of the district court all cases pending action by the
18 district court after the effective date of the resolution.
19 4. On the effective date of transfer, all proceedings relating to ordinance violations must
20 be within the jurisdiction of the district court. Judgments of courts which cease to exist
21 on the effective date of the resolution continue in effect and the district court may
22 enforce the judgments. Fines, forfeitures, and costs due and unpaid on the effective
23 date of the transfer and those which are subsequently collected on cases pending on
24 the effective date of the transfer must be collected by the district court and remitted to
25 the city.
26 5. The authority, duties, powers, jurisdiction, and term of the municipal judge terminates
27 on the last day of the month in which all municipal cases have been transferred to the
28 district court or the expiration of the judge's term, whichever occurs first.
