Section 1:
25.1355.04000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
SECOND ENGROSSMENT
with House Amendments
Introduced by
Senators Wanzek, Myrdal, Thomas
Representatives Brandenburg, Beltz, Kempenich
(Approved by the Delayed Bills Committee)
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact three new sections to chapter 54-40.1 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to a rural catalyst committee, grant program, and fund; to amend and
3 reenact section 54-40.1-02 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to definitions for regional
4 planning councils; to provide an appropriation; and to provide for a transfer.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
6 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 54-40.1-02 of the North Dakota Century Code is
7 amended and reenacted as follows:
8 54-40.1-02. Definitions.
9 In this chapter, unless the context or subject matter otherwise requires:
10 1. "City" means any city incorporated under the laws of this state.
11 2. "Commissioner" means the commissioner of the department.
12 3. "Department" means the department of commerce.
13 4. "Governing body" means the city council or the board of city commissioners or the
14 board of county commissioners.
15 3.5. "Industry" includes agriculture and business.
16 4.6. "Member-at-large" means a person who represents the general citizenry of the county.
17 5. "Minority group" means any identifiable group of people, regardless of numerical size,
18 whose members are denied or limited in employment, education, or training
19 opportunities because of sex, race, creed, color, religion, national origin, or low
20 income.
Page No. 1 25.1355.04000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 6.7. "Organized local development corporation" means any group organized for the
2 purpose of promoting economic development which has filed for incorporation as such
3 with the secretary of state.
4 7.8. "Region" means the area delineated by executive order of the governor.
5 8.9. "Regional comprehensive plan" means a long-range guide for the economic, physical,
6 and social development of a region which identifies regional goals, objectives, and
7 opportunities and embodies the policies of the regional council.
8 9.10. "Regional council" means the council for comprehensive planning and development
9 established in each region pursuant to this chapter.
10 10.11. "Units of general local government" means cities, counties, and organized townships.
11 SECTION 2. A new section to chapter 54-40.1 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
12 and enacted as follows:
13 Rural catalyst committee - Membership - Meetings.
14 1. The rural catalyst committee is created to assist communities in the state with quality
15 of life improvement and economic development projects . The committee includes :
16 a. One individual from each regional council, appointed by the executive boards of
17 the regional councils;
18 b. Two members - a t - large from communities eligible for grants under this chapter or
19 organizations that primarily serve communities eligible for grants under this
20 chapter, appointed by the governor;
21 c. One member of the legislative assembly, appointed by the chairman of legislative
22 management;
23 d. The governor or a designee appointed by the governor; and
24 e. The commissioner or a designee appointed by the commissioner.
25 2. The governor and the commissioner are permanent members of the committee. The
26 term of office of the remaining members is four years. Members may be appointed to
27 additional terms. The governor, or the governor's designee, shall serve as chairman of
28 the committee. The chairman of the committee shall select a vice chairman.
29 3. The committee shall meet at least semiannually to review and approve gran t
30 applications. The committee shall prioritize funding projects that support rura l
31 prosperity and quality of life enhancements.
Page No. 2 25.1355.04000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 4. The committee shall develop guidelines for the rural catalyst grant program, including
2 eligibility criteria and reporting and matching requirements. Grant recipients must
3 provide at least one dollar of matching funds from nonstate sources for every
4 one dollar provided from the rural catalyst fund. The committee may consider
5 matching funds in the form of cash or in - kind contributions .
6 5. The department shall provide administrative support to the committee as needed.
7 6. Members of the committee are entitled to be reimbursed for expenses incurred in the
8 performance of their duties, in the same manner as other state officials are
9 reimbursed, according to sections 44 - 08 - 04 and 54 - 06 - 09, to be paid by the state
10 agency employing the member. A member of the committee who is a member of the
11 legislative assembly is entitled to receive reimbursement for allowable travel expenses
12 and compensation at the rate provided under section 54 - 35 - 10 for each day the
13 member performs official duties of the committee, to be paid by the legislative council.
14 If not otherwise employed by the state of North Dakota, members of the committee are
15 entitled to receive compensation of one hundred thirty-five dollars for each day of
16 attending meetings and performing other duties relating to official business of the
17 committee, to be paid by the department.
18 SECTION 3. A new section to chapter 54-40.1 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
19 and enacted as follows:
20 Rural catalyst grant program - Eligibility - Use of funds - Postaward monitoring -
21 Report to legislative management and legislative assembly.
22 1. The departmen t shall administer the rural catalyst grant program to support quality o f
23 life improvement and economic development projects for rural communities in the
24 state .
25 2. Grant funding may be awarded for projects that will address a critical need in the
26 areas of community and economic development, education, health and wellness, or
27 for the purpose of preserving or reopening rural grocery stores in a rural community
28 with a population of four thousand five hundred or fewer individuals. The maximum
29 grant award under the program is five hundred thousand dollars. At least fifty percen t
30 of the grant funds must be allocated to communities with populations of fewer than
31 one thousand five hundred individuals .
Page No. 3 25.1355.04000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 3. By September first of each even - numbered year, the department shall submit a repor t
2 to the legislative management regarding the results of the program, including the
3 number of projects requested and supported through the program, information on
4 projects receiving funding, matching funds raised for each project, and how the project
5 has affected local communities. The department shall provide this report to the
6 appropriations committees of the next legislative assembly.
7 4. The department shall maintain a database on the department's website regarding data
8 and the status of each approved project.
9 SECTION 4. A new section to chapter 54-40.1 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
10 and enacted as follows:
11 Rural catalyst fund.
12 The rural catalyst fund is a special fund in the state treasury administered by the
13 department. Moneys in the fund must be used for providing grants pursuant to this chapter ,
14 subject to legislative appropriations. Interest earned on moneys in the fund must be credited to
15 the fund.
16 SECTION 5. TRANSFER - STRATEGIC INVESTMENT AND IMPROVEMENTS FUND TO
17 RURAL CATALYST FUND. The office of management and budget shall transfer $5,000,000
18 from the strategic investment and improvements fund to the rural catalyst fund during the
19 biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027.
20 SECTION 6. APPROPRIATION - DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE - RURAL CATALYST
21 GRANT PROGRAM - ONE-TIME FUNDING. There is appropriated out of any moneys in the
22 rural catalyst fund in the state treasury, not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $5,000,000, or
23 so much of the sum as may be necessary, to the department of commerce for the purpose of
24 the rural catalyst grant program, for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30,
25 2027. The appropriation in this section is considered a one-time funding item.
