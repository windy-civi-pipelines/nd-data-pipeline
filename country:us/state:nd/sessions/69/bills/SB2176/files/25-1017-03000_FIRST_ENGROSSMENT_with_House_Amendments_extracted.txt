Section 1:
25.1017.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
with House Amendments
Introduced by
Senators Axtman, Hogan
Representatives O'Brien, Pyle
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new section to chapter 54-07 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to the children's cabinet and designated working groups; to repeal
3 section 50-06-43.1 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to the children's cabinet; and to
4 provide for a legislative management report.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
6 SECTION 1. A new section to chapter 54-07 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
7 and enacted as follows:
8 Children's cabinet - Membership - Duties - Working groups - Report.
9 1. The children's cabinet is created to assess, guide, and coordinate the care for children
10 and families across the state's branches of government and the tribal nations.
11 2. The children's cabinet is composed of:
12 a. The governor, or the governor's designee;
13 b. The chief justice of the supreme court, or the chief justice's designee;
14 c. Two members of the house of representatives, appointed by the majority leader
15 of the house of representatives to serve two-year terms;
16 d. Two members of the senate, appointed by the majority leader of the senate to
17 serve two-year terms;
18 e. The superintendent of public instruction, or the superintendent's designee;
19 f. The director of the committee on protection and advocacy, or the director's
20 designee;
21 g. The commissioner of the department of health and human services, or the
22 commissioner's designee;
Page No. 1 25.1017.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 h. A representative of the tribal nations in the state, who is appointed by the
2 governor; and
3 i. Five individuals, appointed by the governor to serve a term of two years at the
4 pleasure of the governo r , consisting of :
5 (1) A local school representative;
6 (2) A local special education representative;
7 (3) A human service zone director;
8 (4) A representative of a private agency that provides direct services to
9 vulnerable children; and
10 (5) A parent.
11 3. The governor, in collaboration with the chairman of the legislative management, shall
12 appoint as the presiding officer of the children's cabinet a member of the governor's
13 cabinet or a legislator serving on the children's cabinet. The children's cabinet shall
14 select one of its members to serve as the vice-presiding officer.
15 4. The children's cabinet shall meet at least quarterly. Additional meetings may be held at
16 the discretion of the presiding officer.
17 5. The children's cabinet shall:
18 a. Coordinate broad-based leadership across programs, agencies, branches of
19 government, and tribal nations to meet the needs of children and families;
20 b. Develop strategies to address gaps or needs regarding early care and education,
21 medical and behavioral health, community, child welfare, and juvenile justice;
22 c. Develop strategies to provide for the full continuum of care in the delivery of
23 services, including promotion, prevention, early identification and intervention,
24 service delivery, and recovery;
25 d. Seek to engage cooperation across public and private service providers;
26 e. Provide a comprehensive vision for how and where children are best served,
27 attending to children and families in a respectful and relevant manner;
28 f. Seek strategies to provide services to children without consideration of prior
29 engagement with juvenile services;
30 g. Provide for the active participation of consumers and providers statewide on
31 advisory committees;
Page No. 2 25.1017.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 h. Receive information and recommendations from the department of health and
2 human services, department of corrections and rehabilitation, and other state
3 agencies;
4 i. Establish and oversee designated working groups to develop policies impacting
5 children and families; and
6 j. Provide an annual report to the legislative management and governor regarding
7 the activities and findings of:
8 (1) The children's cabinet; and
9 (2) Other designated working groups on children and family issues.
10 6. The office of the governor shall provide staffing and administrative services for the
11 children's cabinet and coordinate with other designated working groups on children
12 and family issues.
13 7. a. The presiding officer of the children's cabinet may designate working groups and
14 appoint or remove liaisons to serve on the working groups.
15 b. A member of the children's cabinet or working group who is not a state employee
16 is entitled to reimbursement for mileage and expenses as provided by law for
17 state officers and employees, to be paid by the office of the governor.
18 c. A state employee who is a member of the children's cabinet or working group is
19 entitled to receive that employee's regular salary and is entitled to reimbursement
20 for mileage and expenses to be paid by the employing agency.
21 d. A member of the children's cabinet or working group who is a member of the
22 legislative assembly is entitled to receive per diem compensation at the rate
23 provided under section 54 - 35 - 10 for each day performing official duties of the
24 children's cabinet or working group. The legislative council shall pay the per diem
25 compensation and reimbursement for travel and such expenses as provided by
26 law for any member of the children's cabinet or working group who is a member
27 of the legislative assembly.
28 SECTION 2. REPEAL. Section 50-06-43.1 of the North Dakota Century Code is repealed.
