Section 1:
25.0161.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
Introduced by
Appropriations Committee
1 A BILL for an Act to provide an appropriation for defraying the expenses of the game and fish
2 department; and to provide an exemption.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
4 SECTION 1. APPROPRIATION. The funds provided in this section, or so much of the funds
5 as may be necessary, are appropriated from other funds derived from special funds and federal
6 funds, to the game and fish department for the purpose of defraying the expenses of the game
7 and fish department, for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027, as
8 follows:
9 Adjustments or
10 Base Level Enhancements Appropriation
11 Salaries and wages $37,008,098 $0 $37,008,098
12 Operating expenses 17,788,258 0 17,788,258
13 Capital assets 5,400,661 0 5,400,661
14 Grants - game and fish 10,089,976 0 10,089,976
15 Land habitat and deer depredation 27,183,516 0 27,183,516
16 Noxious weed control 725,000 0 725,000
17 Missouri River enforcement 313,869 0 313,869
18 Grants, gifts, and donations 677,970 0 677,970
19 Nongame wildlife conservation 100,000 0 100,000
20 Lonetree reservoir 2,156,714 0 2,156,714
21 Wildlife services 500,000 0 500,000
22 Shooting sports grant program 250,000 0 250,000
23 Aquatic nuisance species program 2,533,452 0 2,533,452
Page No. 1 25.0161.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 Total other funds $104,727,514 $0 $104,727,514
2 Full-time equivalent positions 170.00 0.00 170.00
3 SECTION 2. GRANTS, GIFTS, AND DONATIONS LINE ITEM. The grants, gifts, and
4 donations line item in section 1 of this Act includes up to $100,000 received by the game and
5 fish department for surface damage, easements, or reclamation on department-owned or
6 managed properties as a result of mineral exploration and extraction activities.
7 SECTION 3. MIDTERM CONSERVATION AGREEMENTS. The game and fish department
8 may spend up to $2,777,778 from other funds in the land habitat and deer depredation line item
9 in section 1 of this Act for national fish and wildlife foundation midterm conservation agreements
10 with private landowners. The department shall limit the term of these agreements to a maximum
11 of thirty years. The department shall provide buyback provisions after the fifteenth, twentieth,
12 and twenty-fifth years of the agreement if the property owner desires to withdraw all or a portion
13 of acreage from the lease.
14 SECTION 4. EXEMPTION - LINE ITEM TRANSFERS. Notwithstanding section 54-16-04,
15 the director of the office of management and budget shall transfer up to $2,000,000 of
16 appropriation authority between the operating expenses, capital assets, and grants - game and
17 fish line items in section 1 of this Act as requested by the game and fish department during the
18 biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027. The game and fish department
19 shall notify the legislative council of any transfers made pursuant to this section.
