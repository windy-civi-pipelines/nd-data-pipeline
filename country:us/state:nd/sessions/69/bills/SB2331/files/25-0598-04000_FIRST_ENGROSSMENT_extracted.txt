25.0598.04000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
Introduced by
Senators Boschee, Rummel, Kessel
Representatives Lefor, Steiner
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new section to chapter 54-60 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to the Theodore Roosevelt national park working group; to provide a
3 report; and to provide an appropriation.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
5 SECTION 1. A new section to chapter 54-60 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
6 and enacted as follows:
7 Theodore Roosevelt national park working group - Executive committee - Duties -
8 Report.
9 1. The Theodore Roosevelt national park working group must be chaired by a member of
10 the legislative assembly and consists of:
11 a. One member of the senate and one member of the house of representatives,
12 representing different political parties, appointed by the chairman of legislative
13 management;
14 b. One representative of the parks and recreation department, appointed by the
15 governor;
16 c. One representative from an entity that represents commerce or tourism in the
17 Medora community;
18 d. One representative of the division of tourism, appointed by the governor;
19 e. The agriculture commissioner, or the commissioner's designee;
20 f. One representative from the North Dakota university system with an academic or
21 research background in equine studies, appointed by the governor;
Page No. 1 25.0598.04000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 g. One representative of any nonprofit group located in the state which is
2 established to protect the horses in the Theodore Roosevelt national park,
3 appointed by the governor;
4 h. One representative of the national park service, appointed by the national park
5 service; and
6 i. One representative of the Indian affairs commission, appointed by the governor.
7 2. The working group shall:
8 a. In cooperation with the national park service and the United States government,
9 provide recommendations for managing the population of wild horses in the
10 Theodore Roosevelt national park to remain above two hundred horses;
11 b. Identify and offer opportunities to support and maintain the wild horses within the
12 Theodore Roosevelt national park;
13 c. Promote the wild horses to benefit tourism and to make recommendations to the
14 department;
15 d. Assist the department in carrying out activities to educate the public;
16 e. Meet quarterly; and
17 f. Provide, by July first of each year, a report to the legislative management which
18 includes the working group's detailed plan to implement the requirements of this
19 section.
20 3. The working group may make recommendations for management activities, including
21 birth control, removals, and permanent placement of horses after removal. All
22 management activities must be humane and nonlethal , unless the health or safety of a
23 horse is compromised.
24 4. The working group may enter an agreement with the national park service to
25 implement this section.
26 5. Upon recommendation from the working group, the state may enter an agreement with
27 a private entity to address any issues related to the wild horses in the Theodore
28 Roosevelt national park.
29 SECTION 2. APPROPRIATION - DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE - THEODORE
30 ROOSEVELT NATIONAL PARK WORKING GROUP. There is appropriated out of any moneys
31 in the general fund in the state treasury, not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $49,500, or so
Page No. 2 25.0598.04000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 much of the sum as may be necessary, to the department of commerce for the purpose of
2 administering the Theodore Roosevelt national park working group, for the biennium beginning
3 July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027.
