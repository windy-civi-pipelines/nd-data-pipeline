25.0756.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
Introduced by
Representatives Hagert, Christy, Headland, Mitskog, Nathe, O'Brien, Schreiber-Beck,
Stemen
Senators Barta, Bekkedahl, Sickler, Thomas
1 A BILL for an Act to provide appropriations to the attorney general, the department of career and
2 technical education, the department of health and human services, the agriculture
3 commissioner, and the department of transportation for autonomous technology grants; to
4 provide for a legislative management study; and to provide a report.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
6 SECTION 1. APPROPRIATION - ATTORNEY GENERAL - AUTONOMOUS
7 TECHNOLOGY GRANTS - ONE-TIME FUNDING - REPORT TO LEGISLATIVE
8 MANAGEMENT.
9 1. There is appropriated out of any moneys in the general fund in the state treasury, not
10 otherwise appropriated, the sum of $750,000, or so much of the sum as may be
11 necessary, to the attorney general for the purpose of providing autonomous
12 technology grants to individuals or entities that aid the bureau of criminal investigation
13 in conducting missing person searches, crime scene reconstruction, and law
14 enforcement first response operations, for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and
15 ending June 30, 2027. The funding provided in this section is considered a one-time
16 funding item.
17 2. For purposes of this section, autonomous technology includes uncrewed aircraft
18 systems; autonomous vehicles; or other autonomous systems, processes, or
19 technologies.
20 3. The attorney general shall develop an application process and guidelines for the
21 grants, including eligibility criteria, matching requirements, eligible uses of grant
22 funding, and maximum grant awards. To be eligible for a grant, an individual or entity
23 shall certify to the attorney general that the operation of the autonomous technology is
Page No. 1 25.0756.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 related to conducting missing person searches, crime scene reconstruction, and law
2 enforcement first response operations. The attorney general may require an individual
3 or entity to provide one dollar of matching funds for every four dollars awarded by the
4 state. An individual or entity may use grant funding for autonomous technology
5 operations, contracting for services related to autonomous technology, or other eligible
6 uses as determined by the attorney general.
7 4. During the 2025-26 interim, the attorney general shall provide at least one report to the
8 legislative management regarding the grants, including a list of grant recipients, the
9 amounts awarded, and a description of the use of the grant funding.
10 SECTION 2. APPROPRIATION - DEPARTMENT OF CAREER AND TECHNICAL
11 EDUCATION - AUTONOMOUS TECHNOLOGY GRANTS - ONE-TIME FUNDING - REPORT
12 TO LEGISLATIVE MANAGEMENT.
13 1. There is appropriated out of any moneys in the general fund in the state treasury, not
14 otherwise appropriated, the sum of $750,000, or so much of the sum as may be
15 necessary, to the department of career and technical education for the purpose of
16 providing autonomous technology grants to a workforce training center serving the
17 northwest area of the state for providing workforce training to address workforce
18 shortages in the state, including shortages in the oil and gas industry, and for the
19 development of a drone racing course, for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and
20 ending June 30, 2027. The funding provided in this section is considered a one-time
21 funding item.
22 2. For purposes of this section, autonomous technology includes uncrewed aircraft
23 systems; autonomous vehicles; or other autonomous systems, processes, or
24 technologies.
25 3. The department of career and technical education shall develop an application
26 process and guidelines for the grants, including eligibility criteria, matching
27 requirements, eligible uses of grant funding, and maximum grant awards. To be
28 eligible for a grant, a workforce training center shall certify to the department of career
29 and technical education that the operation of the autonomous technology is related to
30 workforce training to address workforce shortages in the state, including shortages in
31 the oil and gas industry, and the development of a drone racing course. The
Page No. 2 25.0756.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 department of career and technical education may require a workforce training center
2 to provide one dollar of matching funds for every four dollars awarded by the state. A
3 workforce training center may use grant funding for education or training curriculum
4 expenses related to autonomous technology, autonomous technology operations,
5 contracting for services related to autonomous technology, or other eligible uses as
6 determined by the department of career and technical education.
7 4. During the 2025-26 interim, the department of career and technical education shall
8 provide at least one report to the legislative management regarding the grants,
9 including a list of grant recipients, the amounts awarded, and a description of the use
10 of the grant funding.

1 SECTION 6. LEGISLATIVE MANAGEMENT STUDY - AUTONOMOUS TECHNOLOGY.
2 During the 2025-26 interim, the legislative management shall consider studying the costs and
3 benefits of investing in autonomous technology infrastructure in the state. The study must
4 include an analysis of the industries using autonomous technology, future industries that may
5 use autonomous technology, the economic impact of autonomous technology, and the return on
6 investment to the state. The legislative management shall report its findings and
7 recommendations, together with any legislation required to implement the recommendations, to
8 the seventieth legislative assembly.
