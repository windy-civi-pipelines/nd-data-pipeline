Section 1:
25.1191.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
with Senate Amendments
Introduced by
Representatives Maki, Monson, Richter
Senator Schaible
1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact sections 15-20.2-07 and 15.1-09-33.1 of the North
2 Dakota Century Code, relating to the powers and duties of center boards and the authority of a
3 school board to pay a first-year signing bonus for teachers; and to declare an emergency.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
5 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 15-20.2-07 of the North Dakota Century Code is
6 amended and reenacted as follows:
7 15-20.2-07. Powers and duties of center boards.
8 The powers and duties of a center board are as follows:
9 1. To supervise, manage, and control an area career and technology center established
10 by the cooperating school districts pursuant to this chapter.
11 2. To provide career and technical education programs approved by the state board.
12 3. To contract with, employ, or pay personnel to administer the affairs and to teach in the
13 area career and technology center, and to remove for cause any personnel when the
14 interests of the area career and technology center may require it; provided, that
15 personnel employed by a center board shall have the same statutory rights as
16 provided by law for personnel employed by public school districts.
17 4. To lease, acquire, or purchase career and technical education equipment for an area
18 career and technology center.
19 5. To lease, acquire, purchase, or sell career and technical education facilities, including
20 real property, for an area career and technology center; provided, that any purchase or
21 sale of real property must first be approved by two-thirds of the school boards of the
22 participating school districts.

Legislative Assembly
1 6. To receive and administer any private, local, state, or federal funds provided for the
2 operation and maintenance of an area career and technology center.
3 7. To enter into contracts consistent with the other powers and duties provided for by this
4 chapter.
5 8. To accept real or personal property available for distribution by the United States or
6 any of its departments or agencies and also to accept federal grants that may be
7 made available in the field of career and technical education.
8 9. To offer a first-year signing bonus to an individual who is licensed or approved by the
9 education standards and practices board, has signed a contract of employment with
10 the center board, and was not a classroom teacher in the state during the previous
11 school year. A bonus paid under this subsection:
12 a. Is in addition to an amount payable under a negotiated teachers' contract.
13 b. May not be included in the center board's negotiated salary schedule.
14 c. May not be included as salary for continuing contract purposes.
15 SECTION 2. AMENDMENT. Section 15.1-09-33.1 of the North Dakota Century Code is
16 amended and reenacted as follows:
17 15.1-09-33.1. School board authority - Payment of signing bonuses.
18 1. The board of a school district may offer a signing bonus to an individual who:
19 a. Is licensed by the education standards and practices board or approved by the
20 education standards and practices board;
21 b. Has signed a contract of employment in the school district; and
22 c. Has never been employed in the newly assigned role by the board of athe school
23 district.
24 2. The board of a school district may pay to the individual the signing bonus authorized
25 by subsection 1 in:
26 a. One lump sum upon the individual signing a contract of employment; or
27 b. Installments over a period of time not to exceed five years from the date the
28 individual signed a contract of employment.
29 3. A signing bonus paid under this section:
30 a. Is in addition to any amount payable under a negotiated teachers' contract.
31 b. May not be included in the district's negotiated salary schedule.
Page No. 2 25.1191.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 c. May not be included as salary for continuing contract purposes.
2 d. May not be paid until the individual is licensed and qualified for the newly
3 assigned role by the education standards and practices board.
4 e. May not be used for an individual employed the prior year in the same role by
5 another school district in the state until the individual has been employed in the
6 district for two years.
7 SECTION 3. EMERGENCY. This Act is declared to be an emergency measure.
