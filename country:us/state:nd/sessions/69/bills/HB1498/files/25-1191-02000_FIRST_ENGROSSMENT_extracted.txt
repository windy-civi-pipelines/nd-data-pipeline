Section 1:
25.1191.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
Introduced by
Representatives Maki, Monson, Richter
Senator Schaible
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new section to chapter 15.1-09 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to the authority of a school board to pay a first-year signing bonus for
3 teachers; to amend and reenact section 15-20.2-07 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating
4 to the powers and duties of center boards; and to declare an emergency.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
6 SECTION 1. A new section to chapter 15.1-09 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
7 and enacted as follows:
8 School board authority - First-year bonus - Payment.
9 1. The board of a school district may offer a firs t - year signing bonus to an individual who :
10 a. Is licensed or approved by the education standards and practices board;
11 b. Has signed a contract of employment in the district; and
12 c. Was not a classroom teacher in the state during the previous school year.
13 2. A bonus paid under this section:
14 a. Is in addition to an amount payable under a negotiated teachers' contract.
15 b. May not be included in the district's negotiated salary schedule.
16 c. May not be included as salary for continuing contract purposes.
17 SECTION 2. AMENDMENT. Section 15-20.2-07 of the North Dakota Century Code is
18 amended and reenacted as follows:
19 15-20.2-07. Powers and duties of center boards.
20 The powers and duties of a center board are as follows:
21 1. To supervise, manage, and control an area career and technology center established
22 by the cooperating school districts pursuant to this chapter.
Page No. 1 25.1191.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 2. To provide career and technical education programs approved by the state board.
2 3. To contract with, employ, or pay personnel to administer the affairs and to teach in the
3 area career and technology center, and to remove for cause any personnel when the
4 interests of the area career and technology center may require it; provided, that
5 personnel employed by a center board shall have the same statutory rights as
6 provided by law for personnel employed by public school districts.
7 4. To lease, acquire, or purchase career and technical education equipment for an area
8 career and technology center.
9 5. To lease, acquire, purchase, or sell career and technical education facilities, including
10 real property, for an area career and technology center; provided, that any purchase or
11 sale of real property must first be approved by two-thirds of the school boards of the
12 participating school districts.
13 6. To receive and administer any private, local, state, or federal funds provided for the
14 operation and maintenance of an area career and technology center.
15 7. To enter into contracts consistent with the other powers and duties provided for by this
16 chapter.
17 8. To accept real or personal property available for distribution by the United States or
18 any of its departments or agencies and also to accept federal grants that may be
19 made available in the field of career and technical education.
20 9. To offer a first-year signing bonus to an individual who is licensed or approved by the
21 education standards and practices board, has signed a contract of employment with
22 the center board, and was not a classroom teacher in the state during the previous
23 school year. A bonus paid under this subsection:
24 a. Is in addition to an amount payable under a negotiated teachers' contract.
25 b. May not be included in the center board's negotiated salary schedule.
26 c. May not be included as salary for continuing contract purposes.
27 SECTION 3. EMERGENCY. This Act is declared to be an emergency measure.
