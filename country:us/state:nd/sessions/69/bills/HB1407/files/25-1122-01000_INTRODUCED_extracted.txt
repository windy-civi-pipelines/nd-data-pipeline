Section 1:
25.1122.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
Introduced by
Representatives Brandenburg, Grueneich, Headland, Nehring
Senators Luick, Myrdal, Thomas, Wanzek, Conley
1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact subsection 2 of section 39-12-05 and subsection 2 of
2 section 39-12-05.3 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to weight limitations for vehicles
3 on the interstate system and weight limitations for vehicles on highways other than the
4 interstate system.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
6 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Subsection 2 of section 39-12-05 of the North Dakota Century
7 Code is amended and reenacted as follows:
8 2. Subject to the limitations imposed by subsection 1 on tires, wheel, and axle loads, the
9 gross weight of which exceeds that determined by the formula of:
10 W = 500 (LN/ + 12N + 36)
N-1
11 where W equals maximum weight in pounds carried on any group of more than one
12 axle; L equals distance in feet between the extremes of any group of consecutive
13 axles; and N equals number of axles in the group under consideration, except that two
14 consecutive sets of tandem axles may carry a gross load of thirty-four thousand
15 pounds [15422.14 kilograms] each, providing the overall distance between the first
16 and last axles of the consecutive sets of tandem axles is at least thirty-six feet [10.97
17 meters]. The gross weight may not exceed eighty thousandone hundred thirteen
18 thousand pounds [36287.3951255.94 kilograms].
19 SECTION 2. AMENDMENT. Subsection 2 of section 39-12-05.3 of the North Dakota
20 Century Code is amended and reenacted as follows:
21 2. Subject to the limitations imposed by subsection 1 on tires, wheel, and axle loads, a
22 person may not operate on a highway that is not part of the interstate system any
23 vehicle the gross weight of which exceeds that determined by the formula of:
24 W = 500 (LN + 12N + 36)

2 where W equals the maximum gross weight in pounds on any vehicle or combination
3 of vehicles; L equals distance in feet between the two extreme axles of any vehicle or
4 combination of vehicles; and N equals the number of axles of any vehicle or
5 combination of vehicles under consideration. The gross weight on stateall highways
6 may not exceed one hundred five thousand five hundredone hundred thirteen
7 thousand pounds [4785451255.94 kilograms] unless otherwise posted and on all other
8 highways the gross weight may not exceed eighty thousand pounds [36287.39
9 kilograms] unlessor designated by local authorities for highways under their jurisdiction
10 for gross weights not to exceed one hundred five thousand five hundred pounds
11 [47854 kilograms]. Local authorities are encouraged to assess all roads under their
12 jurisdiction and designate the roads for the appropriate weight limits allowed under this
13 subsection.
