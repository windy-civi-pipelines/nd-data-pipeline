1 A BILL for an Act to provide an appropriation for defraying the expenses of the judicial branch;
2 to amend and reenact sections 27-02-02 and 27-05-03 of the North Dakota Century Code,
3 relating to the salaries of justices of the supreme court and salaries of district court judges; to
4 provide for transfers; to provide for a report; and to provide an exemption.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
6 SECTION 1. APPROPRIATION. The funds provided in this section, or so much of the funds
7 as may be necessary, are appropriated out of any moneys in the general fund in the state
8 treasury, not otherwise appropriated, and from other funds derived from special funds and
9 federal funds, to the judicial branch for the purpose of defraying the expenses of the judicial
10 branch, for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027, as follows:

3 SECTION 2. ONE-TIME FUNDING - EFFECT ON BASE BUDGET - REPORT TO THE
4 SEVENTIETH LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY. The following amounts reflect the one-time funding
5 items included in the appropriation in section 1 of this Act which are not included in the entity's
6 base budget for the 2027-29 biennium and which the entity shall report to the appropriations
7 committees of the seventieth legislative assembly regarding the use of this funding:
8 One - Time Funding Description General Fund
9 Information technology equipment lease $874,216
10 Case management software cloud migration 758,000
11 Clerk filing software 1,250,000
12 Court records access system 960,000
13 Cloud-based storage fees 866,100
14 Courtroom audio and video equipment 800,000
15 Cass County courtroom equipment 100,000
16 Total $5,608,316
17 SECTION 3. NEW AND VACANT FTE POOL - BUDGET SECTION REPORT.
18 1. The supreme court may not spend funding from the new and vacant FTE funding pool
19 line item in subdivision 1 of section 1 of this Act, but may transfer funds from this line
20 item to the salaries and wages line items within subdivisions 1 and 2 of this Act, to the
21 guardianship and conservatorship line item within subdivision 1 of this Act, and to the
22 judicial conduction commission and disciplinary board line item within subdivision 3 of
23 this Act, as necessary to provide funding for:
24 a. Filling a new position from the date of hire through the end of the biennium; or
25 b. Salaries and wages if actual salaries and wages savings from vacant positions
26 and employee turnover, adjusted for other uses, are less than the estimate used
27 by the sixty-ninth legislative assembly in the development of the appropriation.
28 2. The supreme court shall report to the budget section regarding the use of funding in
29 the pool, including information on:
30 a. New FTE positions, including the date hired;

Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 b. Vacant FTE positions, including information regarding positions that become
2 vacant and positions filled, salaries and wages savings resulting from vacant
3 positions and employee turnover, and the use of salaries and wages savings for
4 other purposes; and
5 c. Additional salaries and wages funding needed due to savings from vacant
6 positions being less than anticipated.
7 3. If funding in the new and vacant FTE pool line item is insufficient to provide the
8 necessary salaries and wages funding for the biennium, the supreme court may
9 request a deficiency appropriation from the seventieth legislative assembly.
10 SECTION 4. APPROPRIATION. There are appropriated any funds received by the supreme
11 court, district courts, and judicial conduct commission and disciplinary board, not otherwise
12 appropriated, pursuant to federal acts and private gifts, grants, and donations for the purpose
13 as designated in the federal acts or private gifts, grants, and donations for the period beginning
14 July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027.
15 SECTION 5. AMENDMENT. Section 27-02-02 of the North Dakota Century Code is
16 amended and reenacted as follows:
17 27-02-02. Salaries of justices of supreme court.
18 The annual salary of each justice of the supreme court is one hundred seventy-nine
19 thousand three hundred twelve dollars through June 30, 2024, and one hundred eighty-six
20 thousand four hundred eighty-four dollars thereaftertwo hundred two thousand three hundred
21 thirty - five dollars . The chief justice of the supreme court is entitled to receive an additional five
22 thousand seventy-one dollars per annum through June 30, 2024, and five thousand two
23 hundred seventy-four six thousand six hundred eighty - one dollars per annum thereafter.
24 SECTION 6. AMENDMENT. Section 27-05-03 of the North Dakota Century Code is
25 amended and reenacted as follows:
26 27-05-03. Salaries and expenses of district judges.
27 The annual salary of each district judge is one hundred sixty-four thousand five hundred
28 thirty-two dollars through June 30, 2024, and one hundred seventy-one thousand one hundred
29 thirteen dollars thereafter one hundred eighty - three thousand ninety - one dollars . Each district
30 judge is entitled to travel expenses, including mileage and subsistence while engaged in the
31 discharge of official duties outside the city in which the judge's chambers are located. The
Page No. 4 25.0169.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 salary and expenses are payable monthly in the manner provided by law. A presiding judge of a
2 judicial district is entitled to receive an additional four thousand six hundred seventy-fivefive
3 thousand two hundred two dollars per annum through June 30, 2024, and four thousand eight
4 hundred sixty-two dollars thereafter.
5 SECTION 7. EXEMPTION - TRANSFERS. Notwithstanding section 54-16-04, the director
6 of the office of management and budget shall transfer appropriation authority between line items
7 in section 1 of this Act, as requested by the supreme court upon a finding by the court that the
8 nature of the duties of the court and its staff requires the transfers to carry on properly the
9 functions of the judicial branch of government.
