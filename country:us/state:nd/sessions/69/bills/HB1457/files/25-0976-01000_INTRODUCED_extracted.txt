Section 1:
25.0976.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
Introduced by
Representatives Henderson, D. Johnston, Christianson, S. Olson, Rios, Frelich, VanWinkle
Senators Walen, Weston
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new section to chapter 23-12 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to an exemption to required vaccines.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
4 SECTION 1. A new section to chapter 23-12 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
5 and enacted as follows:
6 Required vaccines - Exemption.
7 1. As used in this section, "department" means the department of health and human
8 services.
9 2. The department shall publicize an exemption exists for required vaccines that do not
10 meet the minimal standards under this section.
11 3. Notwithstanding any other law, an individual who is required to receive a vaccine for
12 any purpose, including as a condition of employment, school attendance, or securing a
13 license, certification, or degree, may claim an exemption from the requirement if there
14 is not a vaccine approved by the food and drug administration to fulfill the purpose that
15 meets the following criteria:
16 a. The clinical trial the food and drug administration relied on to approve the vaccine
17 evaluated its safety for at least one year after it was administered against a
18 control group that received only a placebo or another vaccine that meets the
19 requirements of this section;
20 b. The department posts on its website the injuries or disease caused by the
21 vaccine and the rate at which the injury or disease occurs due to the vaccine;
22 c. The risk of permanent disability or death from the vaccine has been proven to be
23 less than that caused by the infection it is intended to prevent; and
Page No. 1 25.0976.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 d. The vaccine manufacturer is liable for any death or serious injury caused by the
2 vaccine.
3 4. The department shall inform the public that an individual may claim the exemption
4 under this section on the individual's own behalf or on behalf of a child or dependent.
5 5. A state agency, political subdivision, board, or commission may not require an
6 individual to meet any condition that is inconsistent with this section to claim the
7 exemption under this section.
