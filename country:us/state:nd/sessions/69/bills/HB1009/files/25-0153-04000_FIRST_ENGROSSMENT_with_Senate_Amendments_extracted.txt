1 SECTION 12. OTHER FUNDS - STRATEGIC INVESTMENT AND IMPROVEMENTS
2 FUND - MATCHING FUNDS - REPORT TO LEGISLATIVE MANAGEMENT. The other funds
3 line item in section 1 of this Act includes the sum of $5,500,000 from the strategic investment
4 and improvements fund, of which:
5 1. $3,000,000 is for the purpose of providing grassland grazing grants to an organization
6 representing cooperative grazing associations in the state. To be eligible for a grant
7 under this program, an organization must provide one dollar of matching funds from
8 nonstate sources for every four dollars of grant funding. An organization that receives
9 a grant under this program may distribute the funding to cooperative grazing
10 associations for eligible infrastructure projects, which must be located on national
11 grasslands within the state. Eligible infrastructure projects include water development;
12 fencing; conservation initiatives; compliance with federal permitting requirements,
13 including fees for professional services; and other projects to enhance wildlife habitat,
14 capture carbon, or increase the health of grasslands. Program participants shall
15 develop and implement a grazing land plan in compliance with local soil conservation
16 district guidance and the plan must be approved by the local soil conservation district.
17 The agriculture commissioner shall establish additional guidelines for the program;
18 2. $2,000,000 is for grants to food pantries for meat and protein related purchases; and
19 3. $500,000 is for autonomous technology grants to individuals or entities in the
20 agriculture industry, as follows:
21 a. For purposes of this subsection, autonomous technology includes uncrewed
22 aircraft systems; autonomous vehicles; or other autonomous systems,
23 processes, or technologies.
24 b. The agriculture commissioner shall develop an application process and
25 guidelines for the grants, including eligibility criteria, matching requirements,
26 eligible uses of grant funding, and maximum grant awards. To be eligible for a
27 grant, an individual or entity shall certify to the agriculture commissioner that the
28 operation of the autonomous technology is related to the inspection, operation,
29 maintenance, or construction of agriculture property or infrastructure. The
30 agriculture commissioner may require an individual or entity to provide one dollar
31 of matching funds for every four dollars awarded by the state. An individual or
Page No. 4 25.0153.04000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 entity may use grant funding for autonomous technology operations, contracting
2 for services related to autonomous technology, or other eligible uses as
3 determined by the agriculture commissioner.
4 c. During the 2025-26 interim, the agriculture commissioner shall provide at least
5 one report to the legislative management regarding the grants, including a list of
6 grant recipients, the amounts awarded, and a description of the use of the grant
7 funding.
8 SECTION 13. TRADE OFFICE - MATCHING FUND REQUIREMENT. The North Dakota
9 trade office line item and the general fund appropriation in section 1 of this Act includes
10 $2,000,000 of funding related to the North Dakota trade office. The agriculture commissioner
11 may spend sixty percent of this amount without requiring any matching funds from the trade
12 office. Any additional amounts may be spent only to the extent the North Dakota trade office
13 provides one dollar of matching funds from private or other public sources for each one dollar
14 provided by the agriculture commissioner. Matching funds may include money spent by
15 businesses or organizations to pay salaries to export assistants, provide training to export
16 assistants, or purchase computer equipment as part of the North Dakota trade office's export
17 assistant program.
18 SECTION 14. WATERBANK PROGRAM - MATCHING FUNDS. The salaries and wages
19 line item in section 1 of this Act includes up to $50,000 from the general fund for matching funds
20 for the North Dakota outdoor heritage fund grant provided for the waterbank program.
21 SECTION 15. AMENDMENT. Section 4.1-01-02 of the North Dakota Century Code is
22 amended and reenacted as follows:
23 4.1-01-02. Salary of agriculture commissioner.
24 The annual salary of the agriculture commissioner is one hundred thirty-one thousand four
25 hundred twenty-three dollars through June 30, 2024, and one hundred thirty-six thousand
26 six hundred eighty one hundred forty thousand seven hundred eighty dollars through June 30 ,
27 2026, and one hundred forty-five thousand three dollars after that date.
28 SECTION 16. WILDLIFE SERVICES PROGRAM - INDIRECT COSTS. The wildlife
29 services line item in section 1 of this Act includes the sum of $1,807,400 for the purpose of
30 defraying the expenses of the wildlife services program during the biennium beginning July 1,
31 2025, and ending June 30, 2027. The agriculture commissioner may not use funding
Page No. 5 25.0153.04000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 appropriated for the program to pay indirect costs as part of the United States department of
2 agriculture animal and plant health inspection service wildlife services program.
3 SECTION 17. EXEMPTION - UNEXPENDED APPROPRIATIONS. The following
4 appropriations are not subject to the provisions of section 54-44.1-11 and may be continued into
5 the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027:
6 1. The sum of $300,000 appropriated from federal funds derived from COVID-19 funds
7 for the specialty crop block grant program during the 2019-21 biennium in section 1 of
8 chapter 27 of the 2021 Session Laws, and continued into the 2021-23 biennium in
9 section 2 of chapter 27 of the 2021 Session Laws and the 2023-25 biennium in
10 section 20 of chapter 41 of the 2023 Session Laws;
11 2. The sum of $5,000,000 appropriated from the strategic investment and improvements
12 fund for grasslands grazing grants in the grants line item in section 1 of chapter 9 of
13 the 2021 Session Laws, and continued into the 2023-25 biennium in section 21 of
14 chapter 41 of the 2023 Session Laws;
15 3. The sum of $660,000 appropriated from the environment and rangeland protection
16 fund for the regional livestock development and planning program in section 2 of
17 chapter 76 of the 2023 Session Laws; and
18 4. The sum of $1,000,000 appropriated from the strategic investment and improvements
19 fund for grasslands grazing grants in the grants line item in section 1 of chapter 41 of
20 the 2023 Session Laws.
