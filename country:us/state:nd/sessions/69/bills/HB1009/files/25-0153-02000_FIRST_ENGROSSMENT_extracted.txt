1 environment and rangeland protection fund to the minor use pesticide fund during the biennium
2 beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027.
3 SECTION 6. OTHER FUNDS - TRANSFER - BANK OF NORTH DAKOTA PROFITS -
4 AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTS UTILIZATION COMMISSION. The other funds line item in
5 section 1 of this Act includes the sum of $3,000,000 which the Bank of North Dakota shall
6 transfer from the Bank's current earnings and undivided profits to the agriculture commissioner
7 for deposit in the agricultural products utilization fund for the purpose of defraying the expenses
8 of the agricultural products utilization commission, for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and
9 ending June 30, 2027.
10 SECTION 7. OTHER FUNDS - ENVIRONMENT AND RANGELAND PROTECTION FUND.
11 The other funds line item in section 1 of this Act includes the sum of $7,803,814 from the
12 environment and rangeland protection fund for the purpose of defraying the expenses of various
13 agriculture commissioner programs.
14 SECTION 8. OTHER FUNDS - GAME AND FISH FUND. The other funds line item in
15 section 1 of this Act includes the sum of $671,918 from the game and fish department operating
16 fund for the purpose of defraying the expenses of various agriculture commissioner programs.
17 SECTION 9. OTHER FUNDS - ABANDONED OIL AND GAS WELL PLUGGING AND
18 SITE RECLAMATION FUND. The other funds line item in section 1 of this Act includes the sum
19 of $500,000 from the abandoned oil and gas well plugging and site reclamation fund, of which
20 $200,000 is for the purpose of defraying the expenses of the pipeline restoration and
21 reclamation program and $300,000 is for the purpose of defraying the expenses of the
22 post-production royalty oversight program.
23 SECTION 10. OTHER FUNDS - STRATEGIC INVESTMENT AND IMPROVEMENTS
24 FUND - GRASSLANDS GRAZING GRANT PROGRAM - ONE-TIME FUNDING - MATCHING
25 FUNDS. The other funds line item in section 1 of this Act includes the sum of $3,000,000 from
26 the strategic investment and improvements fund for the purpose of providing grassland grazing
27 grants to an organization representing cooperative grazing associations in the state. To be
28 eligible for a grant under this program, an organization must provide one dollar of matching
29 funds from nonstate sources for every four dollars of grant funding. An organization that
30 receives a grant under this program may distribute the funding to cooperative grazing
31 associations for eligible infrastructure projects, which must be located on national grasslands
Page No. 3 25.0153.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 within the state. Eligible infrastructure projects include water development; fencing;
2 conservation initiatives; compliance with federal permitting requirements, including fees for
3 professional services; and other projects to enhance wildlife habitat, capture carbon, or increase
4 the health of grasslands. Program participants shall develop and implement a grazing land plan
5 in compliance with local soil conservation district guidance and the plan must be approved by
6 the local soil conservation district. The agriculture commissioner shall establish additional
7 guidelines for the program.
8 SECTION 11. TRADE OFFICE - MATCHING FUND REQUIREMENT. The North Dakota
9 trade office line item and the general fund appropriation in section 1 of this Act include
10 $2,000,000 of funding related to the North Dakota trade office. The agriculture commissioner
11 may spend sixty percent of this amount without requiring any matching funds from the trade
12 office. Any additional amounts may be spent only to the extent the North Dakota trade office
13 provides one dollar of matching funds from private or other public sources for each one dollar
14 provided by the agriculture commissioner. Matching funds may include money spent by
15 businesses or organizations to pay salaries to export assistants, provide training to export
16 assistants, or purchase computer equipment as part of the North Dakota trade office's export
17 assistant program.
18 SECTION 12. WATERBANK PROGRAM - MATCHING FUNDS. The salaries and wages
19 line item in section 1 of this Act includes up to $50,000 from the general fund for matching funds
20 for the North Dakota outdoor heritage fund grant provided for the waterbank program.
21 SECTION 13. AMENDMENT. Section 4.1-01-02 of the North Dakota Century Code is
22 amended and reenacted as follows:
23 4.1-01-02. Salary of agriculture commissioner.
24 The annual salary of the agriculture commissioner is one hundred thirty-one thousand four
25 hundred twenty-three dollars through June 30, 2024, and one hundred thirty-six thousand
26 six hundred eighty one hundred forty thousand seven hundred eighty dollars through June 30 ,
27 2026, and one hundred forty-five thousand three dollars after that date.
28 SECTION 14. EXEMPTION - UNEXPENDED APPROPRIATIONS. The following
29 appropriations are not subject to the provisions of section 54-44.1-11 and may be continued into
30 the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027:
Page No. 4 25.0153.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 1. The sum of $300,000 appropriated from federal funds derived from COVID-19 funds
2 for the specialty crop block grant program during the 2019-21 biennium in section 1 of
3 chapter 27 of the 2021 Session Laws, and continued into the 2021-23 biennium in
4 section 2 of chapter 27 of the 2021 Session Laws and the 2023-25 biennium in
5 section 20 of chapter 41 of the 2023 Session Laws; and
6 2. The sum of $660,000 appropriated from the environment and rangeland protection
7 fund for the regional livestock development and planning program in section 2 of
8 chapter 76 of the 2023 Session Laws.
