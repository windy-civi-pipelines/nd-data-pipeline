1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact sections 16.1-03-01, 16.1-03-07, 16.1-03-11,
2 16.1-03-14, and 16.1-03-22 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to political
3 organizations; and to repeal sections 16.1-03-03, 16.1-03-05, 16.1-03-08, and 16.1-03-17 of the
4 North Dakota Century Code, relating to district parties and district committees.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
6 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 16.1-03-01 of the North Dakota Century Code is
7 amended and reenacted as follows:
8 16.1-03-01. Party caucus - Time and manner of holding - Caucus call - Notice.
9 1. Each legislative district party shall organize in conformance with the state legislative
10 district boundaries as established by the legislative assembly and as set forth under
11 chapter 54-03.
12 2. On or before May fifteenth following the last preceding general election and after
13 redistricting of the legislative assembly becomes effective, a party caucus must be
14 held by every legislative district party. The legislative district party may organize the
15 caucus by precinct or on an at-large basis for the entire district.
16 3. The legislative district chairman of each party shall set the date and time for the party
17 caucus. If there is not a duly elected district chairman in a legislative district, the state
18 party executive committee may issue the call for the caucus. The call must contain the
19 following:
20 a. Name of party.
21 b. Legislative district number.
22 c. Date of caucus.
23 d. Place of caucus.
24 e. Hours of caucus.

13 before the most recent redistricting; or
14 b. A district with new geographic area has a population within the new geographic
15 area more than twenty - five percent of the district's total population as determined
16 in the most recent federal decennial census.
17 SECTION 2. AMENDMENT. Section 16.1-03-07 of the North Dakota Century Code is
18 amended and reenacted as follows:
19 16.1-03-07. Meeting of districtDistrict committee - Organization.
20 1. If a legislative district chooses to organize by precinct in every odd-numbered year, the
21 district committee of each party shall meet within fifteen days after the caucus
22 provided for in section 16.1-03-01. The day, hour, and site must be set by the existing
23 district committee chairman. Any incumbent members of the legislative assembly from
24 the party and any other individual provided for by the district committee's bylaws
25 constitute the district committee of the party. TheAt each party caucus, the district
26 committee of a party must be organized to coincide with the geographical boundary
27 lines of state legislative districts. Each member of any committee provided for in this
28 chapter must be a qualified elector.
29 2. The district committee shall select the officers of the district committee and forward to
30 the state committee the name and contact information of the district committee
31 chairman. The district committee may appoint an executive committee consistent with

5 3. If the office of chairman becomes vacant, the vacancy may be filled as provided by the

7 4. The bylaws of the state committee or state party may not include any requirement
8 providing directives or procedures for the method of the organization of district
9 committees nor may the state committee or state party take any action or impose any
10 requirement regarding district party organization unless a district lacks a district
11 committee that is able to carry out the responsibilities under this chapter. A state party
12 may not take any action that is inconsistent with this chapter.
13 5. After redistricting of the legislative assembly becomes effective, the state party chair
14 may appoint a temporary district party organization chair in any newly established
15 district or a district that lacks a district committee able to carry out the responsibilities
16 of this chapter. The temporary district party organization chair shall organize the
17 district political party as closely as possible in conformance with this chapter to assure
18 compliance with primary election filing deadlines.

1 SECTION 4. AMENDMENT. Section 16.1-03-14 of the North Dakota Century Code is
2 amended and reenacted as follows:
3 16.1-03-14. State party convention.
4 1. The state party state committeechairman shall set the place and time of the state party
5 convention to be held in each general election year. Subject to party rules and bylaws,
6 the state party convention may:
7 a. Adopt rules to govern a convention held under this section.
8 b. Nominate the legal number of elector nominees and alternate elector nominees
9 for its party for the offices of presidential electors. The nominees must be
10 qualified electors of this state.
11 b.c. Elect the required number of delegates and alternates to the national party
12 convention.
13 c.d. Endorse candidates as provided under subsection 2.
14 2. The candidate or candidates for endorsement or election must be declared endorsed
15 or elected under the rules of the party, and the chairman and secretary of the
16 convention shall issue certificates of endorsement as provided in section 16.1-11-06 or
17 certificates of election. The names of the qualified electors nominated for presidential
18 electors the party wishes to place on the general election ballot must be certified by
19 the chairman and secretary of the convention to the secretary of state by four p.m. on
20 the sixty-fourth day before the general election to be placed upon the general election
21 ballot as provided in section 16.1-06-07.1.
22 SECTION 5. AMENDMENT. Section 16.1-03-22 of the North Dakota Century Code is
23 amended and reenacted as follows:
24 16.1-03-22. Liability of officers and members.
25 1. Officers and executive committee membersof a political party organization are immune
26 from civil liability for any act or omission relating to their service or function as an
27 officer or executive committee member, unless the act or omission constitutes gross or
28 willful negligence or gross or willful misconduct.
29 2. Members of a political organization are neither obligated to pay, nor liable upon, any
30 political organization obligation.
