1 A BILL for an Act to provide an appropriation for defraying the expenses of the parks and
2 recreation department; to create and enact a new section to chapter 55-08 of the North Dakota
3 Century Code, relating to the creation and naming of state parks; to provide for a transfer; to
4 provide for a report; and to provide an exemption.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
6 SECTION 1. APPROPRIATION. The funds provided in this section, or so much of the funds
7 as may be necessary, are appropriated out of any moneys in the general fund in the state
8 treasury, not otherwise appropriated, and from other funds derived from special funds and
9 federal funds, to the parks and recreation department for the purpose of defraying the expenses
10 of the parks and recreation department and for providing a grant to the International Peace
11 Garden, for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027, as follows:

4 SECTION 3. NEW AND VACANT FTE POOL - LIMITATION - TRANSFER REQUEST. The
5 parks and recreation department may not spend funds appropriated in the new and vacant FTE
6 pool line item in section 1 of this Act, but may request the office of management and budget to
7 transfer funds from the new and vacant FTE pool line item to other line items with salaries and
8 wages in accordance with the guidelines and reporting provisions included in House Bill
9 No. 1015, as approved by the sixty-ninth legislative assembly.
10 SECTION 4. ADDITIONAL INCOME - APPROPRIATION - REPORTING. In addition to the
11 amounts appropriated in section 1 of this Act, any additional federal or special funds that
12 become available are appropriated to the parks and recreation department, for the biennium
13 beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027. The department shall report any additional
14 income under this section to the office of management and budget and the legislative council.
15 SECTION 5. TRANSFER - GAME AND FISH DEPARTMENT TO PARKS AND
16 RECREATION DEPARTMENT - GAME AND FISH OPERATING FUND - BOAT RAMP
17 OPERATION AND MAINTENANCE. The sum of $122,000, or so much of the sum as may be
18 necessary, included in the park operations and maintenance line item in subdivision 1 of
19 section 1 of this Act, is from the game and fish operating fund, or federal or other funds
20 available to the game and fish department, which must be transferred to the parks and
21 recreation department for maintenance, operating, and extraordinary repairs expenses relating
22 to boat ramps at state parks, for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30,
23 2027.
24 SECTION 6. OTHER FUNDS - STRATEGIC INVESTMENT AND IMPROVEMENTS FUND.
25 The other funds line item in section 1 of this Act includes $19,941,500 from the strategic
26 investment and improvements fund, of which:
27 1. $12,500,000 is for deferred maintenance at state parks, $924,000 is for comfort
28 stations at state parks, $2,500,000 is for state parks technology enhancements,
29 $800,000 is for a Lake Metigoshe state park sewer infrastructure project, $1,250,000
30 is for state parks matching funds, $1,400,000 is for an international music camp
31 complex revitalization project at the International Peace Garden, $250,000 is for a

1 sewer, water, and irrigation master development plan at the International Peace
2 Garden, and $317,500 is for a cactus conservatory project at the International Peace
3 Garden.
4 2. The parks and recreation department may use funding appropriated for deferred
5 maintenance at state parks for the purchase of buildings to replace currently owned
6 buildings that are beyond feasible repair.
7 3. The parks and recreation department shall use the $800,000 identified in this section
8 for the Lake Metigoshe state park sewer infrastructure project to provide a grant to a
9 local sewer district to replace or repair the sewer infrastructure system servicing Lake
10 Metigoshe state park and surrounding areas. Grant funding awarded under this
11 section may not exceed twenty percent of the total project cost to replace or repair the
12 sewer infrastructure system.
13 SECTION 7. INTERNATIONAL PEACE GARDEN. The International Peace Garden line
14 item in section 1 of this Act includes $1,967,500 for projects at the International Peace Garden,
15 which the parks and recreation department may provide only to the extent the funds are
16 matched by the province of Manitoba at a value equal to the United States dollar exchange rate
17 on July 1, 2025.
18 SECTION 8. A new section to chapter 55-08 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
19 and enacted as follows:
20 Creation and naming of state parks.
21 Notwithstanding any other provision of law, a state agency or official may not create a new
22 state park or rename an existing state park without prior approval from the legislative assembly.
23 SECTION 9. EXEMPTION - UNEXPENDED APPROPRIATIONS. The following
