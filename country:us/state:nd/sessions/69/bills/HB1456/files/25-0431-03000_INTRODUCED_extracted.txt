25.0431.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
Introduced by
Representatives S. Olson, Schauer, K. Anderson, McLeod, Meier, Rohr, Steiner
Senators Boehm, Clemens, Larson, Paulson, Weston
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new section to chapter 15.1-07 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to school chaplains in public school districts.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
4 SECTION 1. A new section to chapter 15.1-07 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
5 and enacted as follows:
6 Certified chaplains - School district policy - Requirements.
7 1. As used in this section, "certified chaplain" means a chaplain certified by a national or
8 state chaplaincy organization or ecclesiastically endorsed by a religious organization.
9 2. A school district may employ or accept as a volunteer a certified chaplain to provide
10 support, services, and programs for a student, staff member, or legal guardian of a
11 student as authorized by the board of a school district.
12 3. A certified chaplain employed or volunteering under this section is not required to be
13 licensed by the education standards and practices board.
14 4. The board of a school district shall obtain a criminal history record check from the
15 bureau of criminal investigation for each individual hired or volunteering under this
16 section. All costs associated with a background check are the responsibility of the
17 board under section 12 - 60 - 24 .
18 5. A school district may not employ or accept as a volunteer under this section an
19 individual who is required to register under section 12.1 - 32 - 15 .
20 6. A cause of action does not arise against a certified chaplain for an action taken or
21 statement made while providing support, services, or programs for a student, staff
22 member, or legal guardian of a student, unless the action or statement was
23 maliciously, willfully, and deliberately intended to cause harm to, harass, or intimidate
Page No. 1 25.0431.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 an individual seeking the support, services, or programs provided by the certified
2 chaplain.
3 SECTION 2. APPROPRIATION - DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC INSTRUCTION -
4 KINDERGARTEN THROUGH GRADE TWELVE SCHOOL CHAPLAINS. There is appropriated
5 out of any moneys in the general fund in the state treasury, not otherwise appropriated, the sum
6 of $500,000, or so much of the sum as may be necessary, to the department of public
7 instruction for the purpose of funding the salaries, training, oversight, and governance of school
8 chaplains and evaluating the efficacy of the program, for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025,
9 and ending June 30, 2027. The superintendent of public instruction may provide each school
10 district no more than $200,000.
