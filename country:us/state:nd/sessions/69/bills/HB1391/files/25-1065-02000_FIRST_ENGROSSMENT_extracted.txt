8 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 14-02.4-01 of the North Dakota Century Code is
9 amended and reenacted as follows:
10 14-02.4-01. State policy against discrimination.
11 It is the policy of this state to prohibit discrimination on the basis of race, color, religion, sex,
12 national origin, age, the presence of any mental or physical disability, health status, status with
13 regard to marriage or public assistance, or participation in lawful activity off the employer's
14 premises during nonworking hours which is not in direct conflict with the essential
15 business-related interests of the employer; to prevent and eliminate discrimination in
16 employment relations, public accommodations, housing, state and local government services,
17 and credit transactions; and to deter those who aid, abet, or induce discrimination or coerce
18 others to discriminate.

3 SECTION 3. AMENDMENT. Section 14-02.4-03 of the North Dakota Century Code is
4 amended and reenacted as follows:
5 14-02.4-03. Employer's discriminatory practices.
6 1. It is a discriminatory practice for an employer to fail or refuse to hire an individual; to
7 discharge an employee; or to accord adverse or unequal treatment to an individual or
8 employee with respect to application, hiring, training, apprenticeship, tenure,
9 promotion, upgrading, compensation, layoff, or a term, privilege, or condition of
10 employment, because of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, physical or
11 mental disability, health status, status with respect to marriage or public assistance, or
12 participation in lawful activity off the employer's premises during nonworking hours
13 which is not in direct conflict with the essential business-related interests of the
14 employer.
15 2. It is a discriminatory practice for an employer to fail or refuse to make reasonable
16 accommodations for an otherwise qualified individual with a physical or mental
17 disability, because that individual is pregnant, or because of that individual's religion,
18 or because of that individual's health status. An employer is not required to provide an
19 accommodation that would disrupt or interfere with the employer's normal business
20 operations; threaten an individual's health or safety; contradict a business necessity of
21 the employer; or impose an undue hardship on the employer, taking into consideration
22 the size of the employer's business, the type of business, the financial resources of
23 the employer, and the estimated cost and extent of the accommodation. For purposes
24 of this subsection, "pregnant" includes pregnancy, childbirth, and related medical
25 conditions.
26 3. This chapter does not prohibit compulsory retirement of any employee who has
27 attained sixty-five years of age, but not seventy years of age, and who, for the
28 two-year period immediately before retirement, is employed in a bona fide executive or
29 high policymaking position, if the employee is entitled to an immediate nonforfeiture
30 annual retirement benefit from a pension, profit-sharing, savings, or deferred

18 condition of employment because of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, physical or

28 nonworking hours which activity is not in direct conflict with the essential business-related

12 SECTION 13. A new section to chapter 14-02.4 of the North Dakota Century Code is
13 created and enacted as follows:
14 Health status inquiry not discriminatory.
15 1. A health care facility, licensed under chapter 23 - 16, does not unlawfully discriminate
16 under this chapter if the health care facility:
17 a. Asks an employee to volunteer specific health status information for the purpose
18 of determining whether the health care facility should implement reasonable
19 accommodation measures to protect the safety and health of employees,
20 patients, visitors, and other individuals from communicable diseases; and
21 b. Implements reasonable accommodation measures for employees, patients,
22 visitors, and other individuals to protect the health and safety of individuals from
23 communicable diseases.
24 2. A health care facility may consider an employee who declines to provide health status
25 information to be at risk for a communicable disease for purposes of determining
26 whether reasonable accommodation measures must be implemented.
