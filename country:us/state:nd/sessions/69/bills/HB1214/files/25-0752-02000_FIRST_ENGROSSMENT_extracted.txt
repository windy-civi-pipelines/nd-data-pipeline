Section 1:
25.0752.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
Introduced by
Representatives Richter, Heinert, Novak, J. Olson, Sanford, Murphy, Jonas
Senators Barta, Rummel, Schaible, Sickler
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new section to chapter 15.1-27 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to transportation weighted student unit equivalents; to amend and
3 reenact section 15.1-31-05 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to open enrollment
4 transportation; and to repeal sections 15.1-27-26.1, 15.1-27-27.1, 15.1-27-28.1, 15.1-27-30.1,
5 and 15.1-27-31.1 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to payments for school district
6 transportation of students, special education students, and career and technical education
7 students, the distribution of transportation payments in the event of school district closure, and
8 state transportation payments to school districts.
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
10 SECTION 1. A new section to chapter 15.1-27 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
11 and enacted as follows:
12 Transportation average daily membership equivalents converted to weighted student
13 unit equivalents - Determination.
14 1. The superintendent of public instruction shall determine transportation average daily
15 membership equivalents for eligible school districts providing schoolbus transportation
16 in contract schoolbuses, or in district-owned and operated schoolbuses, and for
17 eligible school districts with students riding commercial buses to and from a school
18 within the incorporated limits of a city. Transportation average daily membership
19 equivalents must be determined as follows:
20 a. 0.0011 multiplied by the number of eligible large bus miles;
21 b. 0.00052 multiplied by the number of eligible small bus miles;
22 c. 0.01 multiplied by the number of eligible large bus runs;
Page No. 1 25.0752.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 d. 0.00468 multiplied by the number of eligible small bus runs;
2 e. 0.012 multiplied by the number of square miles in the school district;
3 f. 2.0 multiplied by the number of educational school plants within the school district
4 boundary; and
5 g. 0.00025 multiplied by the number of eligible miles transported by a student's
6 family and reimbursed by the school district.
7 2. The superintendent of public instruction shall determine each school district's total
8 transportation average daily membership equivalent by adding the products derived
9 under subsection 1 and then multiplying the total transportation average daily
10 membership equivalent by 0.1 to determine the school district's weighted student unit
11 equivalent.
12 3. Subject to legislative appropriation, the superintendent of public instruction shall pay
13 each school district providing school bus transportation in contract schoolbuses, or in
14 district-owned and operated schoolbuses, an amount equal to the school district's
15 weighted student unit equivalent multiplied by the per student payment rate for the
16 applicable school year in subsection 3 of section 15.1 - 27 - 04.1 .
17 4. To be eligible, school districts must certify compliance with state law relating to
18 schoolbus standards and school vehicle driver requirements. The superintendent of
19 public instruction may adopt rules governing certification of compliance under this
20 subsection.
21 5. Each year, the superintendent of public instruction shall determine the school district's
22 transportation average daily membership equivalent based on the latest available
23 miles and runs. Eligible miles and runs include miles and runs transporting eligible
24 students to and from:
25 a. School, including miles students are transported by family transportation in
26 accordance with section 15.1 - 30 - 02 ;
27 b. Special education programs approved by the superintendent of public instruction,
28 regardless of whether the students transported live within the incorporated limits
29 of the city in which the students' school is located, including miles students are
30 transported by family transportation pursuant to subsection 5; and
Page No. 2 25.0752.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 c. Career and technology centers and career and technical education courses,
2 either within the district or in other school districts offered through cooperative
3 arrangements approved by the state board for career and technical education,
4 regardless of whether the students transported live within the incorporated limits
5 of the city in which the students' school is located.
6 6. To be eligible for special education miles, a student transported by a family member
7 must:
8 a. Be a student with a disability as defined in chapter 15.1 - 32 ;
9 b. Have an individualized education plan that requires the student attend a public or
10 nonpublic school located outside the student's school district of residence; and
11 c. Be transported by an adult family member in family provided transportation
12 reimbursed by the school district for no more than two round trips per day
13 between the home of the student and the school.
14 7. If a school district ceases to exist, the superintendent of public instruction shall adjust,
15 in the first year after closure, the transportation average daily membership equivalents
16 of each school district in the state which enrolls students who attended the former
17 school. The superintendent of public instruction shall determine the transportation
18 average daily membership equivalents the former school district would have been
19 entitled to for providing transportation services during the school district's final year of
20 operation and shall prorate those transportation average daily membership
21 equivalents to each enrolling school district based on the number of students enrolled
22 from the former school. Transportation average daily membership equivalents of the
23 enrolling school districts after the first year will be based on reported transportation
24 average daily membership equivalents.
25 8. This section does not apply to miles and runs:
26 a. Incurred in providing transportation for students to attend extracurricular activities
27 or events; or
28 b. Related to students who are participating in open enrollment, or who are enrolled
29 pursuant to a written contract entered by the sending and admitting districts
30 which waives the charge and collection of tuition for the student.
Page No. 3 25.0752.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 9. The superintendent of public instruction may adopt rules relating to information
2 reported by school districts and reporting deadlines.
3 SECTION 2. AMENDMENT. Section 15.1-31-05 of the North Dakota Century Code is
4 amended and reenacted as follows:
5 15.1-31-05. Open enrollment - Transportation.
6 A school district of residence may provide transportation to a student participating in open
7 enrollment. If a district of residence does not provide transportation to a student participating in
8 open enrollment, transportation may be provided by the admitting district. A school district may
9 not receive transportation aid disbursements under subdivision a of subsection 1 of section
10 15.1-27-26.1 or subsection 3 of section 15.1-27-26.1 forinclude any factors associated with
11 transporting students who are participating in open enrollment, or who are enrolled pursuant to
12 a written contract entered by the sending and admitting districts which waives the charge and
13 collection of tuition for the student in the calculation of transportation average daily membership
14 equivalents under section 2 of this Act.
15 SECTION 3. REPEAL. Sections 15.1-27-26.1, 15.1-27-27.1, 15.1-27-28.1, 15.1-27-30.1,
16 and 15.1-27-31.1 of the North Dakota Century Code are repealed.
