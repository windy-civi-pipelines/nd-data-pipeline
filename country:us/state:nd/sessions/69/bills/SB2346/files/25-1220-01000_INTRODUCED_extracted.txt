Section 1:
25.1220.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
Introduced by
Senators Erbele, Thomas, Lemm
Representatives D. Anderson, Nelson, Schreiber-Beck
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new section to chapter 4.1-03 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to beef commission member appointments; and to amend and reenact
3 section 4.1-03-02 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to applicants for membership on
4 the beef commission.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
6 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 4.1-03-02 of the North Dakota Century Code is
7 amended and reenacted as follows:
8 4.1-03-02. North Dakota beef commission - Membership - Qualifications.
9 1. The North Dakota beef commission consists of:
10 a. Three beef producers;
11 b. One cattle feeder;
12 c. One dairy producer;
13 d. One representative of a public livestock market; and
14 e. Three at-large representatives.
15 2. The governor shall appoint:
16 a. Each beef producer from a list of at least two names submitted by the North
17 Dakota stockmen's association;
18 b. The cattle feeder from a list of at least two names submitted by the North Dakota
19 stockmen's association feeder council;
20 c. The dairy producer from a list of at least two names submitted by the milk
21 producers association of North Dakota;
22 d. The representative of a public livestock market from a list of at least two names
23 submitted by the North Dakota livestock marketing association; and
24 e. The three at-large representatives.

1 3. Each individual seeking membership as an a t - large representative shall complete and
2 submit an application in accordance with section 2 of this Act. In a year in which an
3 a t - large representative term is scheduled to expire, the application period opens on
4 March first and closes on May first.
5 4. a. Each member of the commission must:
6 (1) Be a United States citizen and a resident of this state;
7 (2) Be actively engaged in that phase of the cattle industry the member
8 represents; and
9 (3) Have been actively engaged in that phase of the cattle industry for a period
10 of five years.
11 b. Each member of the commission, except the representative of a public livestock
12 market, must be a participating producer.
13 c. For purposes of this subsection, "actively engaged" means that the individual:
14 (1) Has an ownership interest in an operation that is of sufficient scope and
15 significance as to constitute a distinct activity; and
16 (2) Has and regularly exercises direct control of the operation.
17 SECTION 2. A new section to chapter 4.1-03 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
18 and enacted as follows:
19 At - large representatives - Expiring terms - Notice .
20 1. When the term of an a t - large representative is scheduled to expire, the commission
21 shall publish a notification, beginning in March of that year, once per month for two
22 consecutive months in the official newspaper of each county. The notification must
23 include:
24 a. A statement indicating applications are being accepted for an a t - large
25 representative member on the commission;
26 b. Instructions for obtaining an application form;
27 c. The deadline to submit an application;
28 d. Submission instructions for a completed application; and
29 e. The length of the term of office.
30 2. Beginning on March first, the commission shall post on the commission's website a
31 notification that includes the information provided in subsection 1 in a year when the
