18 department of corrections and rehabilitation is unsuccessful in obtaining a federal grant of

5 SECTION 7. MISSOURI RIVER CORRECTIONAL CENTER PLANNING AND DESIGN -
6 STEERING COMMITTEE - LEGISLATIVE INTENT - REPORT. The appropriation in the adult
7 services line item in section 1 of this Act includes $20,000,000 from the strategic investment
8 and improvements fund for planning and design for a new minimum security male correctional
9 facility for the Missouri River correctional center. The department of corrections and
10 rehabilitation shall establish a Missouri River correctional center steering committee to oversee
11 the design and construction of the new Missouri River correctional center facility, including other
12 options, for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027. The committee
13 must include one member of the senate appointed by the senate majority leader, one member
14 of the house appointed by the house majority leader, and one member of the minority party from
15 either the senate or the house appointed by the minority leaders of the senate and the house. It
16 is the intent of the sixty-ninth legislative assembly that the seventieth legislative assembly
17 consider supporting the completion of a new minimum security facility for the Missouri River
18 correctional center during the biennium beginning July 1, 2027, and ending June 30, 2029. The
19 department of corrections and rehabilitation shall provide a quarterly report to the legislative
20 management regarding the planning and design of a new minimum security facility.
21 SECTION 8. LEGISLATIVE MANAGEMENT STUDY - DIVERSION AND DEFLECTION
22 CENTERS. During the 2025-26 interim, the legislative management shall consider studying the
23 need for diversion and deflection centers in the state. The study must include the appropriate
24 involvement of the state in the planning and establishment of diversion and deflection centers.
25 The legislative management shall report its findings and recommendations, together with any
26 legislation required to implement the recommendations, to the seventieth legislative assembly.
27 SECTION 9. LEGISLATIVE MANAGEMENT STUDY - SENTENCING, CORRECTIONS,
28 AND PAROLE OVERSIGHT. During the 2025-26 interim, the legislative management shall
29 study the sentencing, correctional, and parole systems within the state, with a focus on
30 improving transparency, consistency, and outcomes in the administration of justice. The study
31 must include a comprehensive review of:
Page No. 4 25.0182.07000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 1. Sentencing requirements and incarceration impacts, including an evaluation of the
2 potential impacts of requiring offenders sentenced to the custody of the department of
3 corrections and rehabilitation to serve eighty-five percent of their terms in secured
4 facilities. The study must include:
5 a. Cost analyses;
6 b. Consideration of data and trends relating to criminal offenses and sentencing;
7 c. Consideration of recidivism trends;
8 d. Consideration of rehabilitation and re-entry opportunities;
9 e. Definitions of terms related to sentencing and incarceration; and
10 f. Safety and accountability measures for minimum security facilities and
11 transitional facilities.
12 2. The structure and function of the parole board, including provisions of chapter 12-59
13 and any rules adopted by the parole board. The study must include:
14 a. Membership structure, appointment process, terms, quorum, and meeting
15 transparency;
16 b. Parole eligibility requirements and determinations;
17 c. Victim rights and notification systems; and
18 d. The role and input of the department of corrections and rehabilitation in
19 parole-related matters.
20 The study may include input from:
21 1. The judicial branch, including district court judges regarding sentencing decisions;
22 2. State's attorney and defense attorneys regarding charging practices, plea
23 negotiations, and trial resources;
24 3. The department of corrections and rehabilitation regarding inmate classification,
25 housing, placement authority, and transitional services, including work or educational
26 release;
27 4. The parole board regarding parole eligibility and release decisions;
28 5. Law enforcement representatives, including the chiefs of police association, sheriff's
29 association, and attorney general's office, regarding impacts to public safety and local
30 and regional jails;
Page No. 5 25.0182.07000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 6. The league of cities and association of counties regarding impacts to public safety and
2 local and regional jails;
3 7. Justice-involved individuals regarding the impact of current and potential sentencing
4 and parole practices; and
5 8. Crime victims regarding the impact of sentencing.
6 The legislative management shall report its findings and recommendations, together with any
7 legislation required to implement the recommendations, to the seventieth legislative assembly.
8 SECTION 10. EXEMPTION - UNEXPENDED APPROPRIATIONS. The following
9 appropriations are not subject to the provisions of section 54-44.1-11 and may be continued into
10 the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027:
11 1. Any amounts remaining from funds transferred to the department of corrections and
12 rehabilitation for deferred maintenance and extraordinary repairs from funding
13 appropriated from the strategic investment and improvements fund to the office of
14 management and budget in section 1 of chapter 640 of the 2023 Special Session
15 Session Laws.
16 2. Any amounts remaining from funds transferred to the department of corrections and
17 rehabilitation for deferred maintenance and extraordinary repairs from funding
18 appropriated from federal funds derived from the state fiscal recovery fund to the office
19 of management and budget in subsection 10 of section 1 of chapter 550 of the 2021
20 Special Session Session Laws and continued pursuant to section 9 of chapter 15 of
21 the 2023 Session Laws.
22 3. The sum of $131,200,000 appropriated from the strategic investment and
23 improvements fund for the construction of a new Heart River correctional center in
24 section 1 of chapter 15 of the 2023 Session Laws.
25 4. The sum of $307,000 appropriated from the strategic investment and improvements
26 fund for DOCSTARS maintenance in section 1 of chapter 15 of the 2023 Session
27 Laws.
28 5. The sum of $2,000,000 appropriated from the strategic investment and improvements
29 fund for information technology needs in section 1 of chapter 15 of the 2023 Session
30 Laws.
