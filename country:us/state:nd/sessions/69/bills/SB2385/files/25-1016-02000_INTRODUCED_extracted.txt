1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new chapter to title 23 and two new sections to chapter
2 23-10 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to receivers for mobile home parks, annual
3 ownership information for mobile home parks, and mobile home park tenant relations; to amend
4 and reenact sections 23-10-03, 23-10-04, 23-10-06, 23-10-06.2, 23-10-12, and 47-10-28 of the
5 North Dakota Century Code, relating to licensure and regulation of mobile home parks; to
6 provide a penalty; and to provide an expiration date.
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
8 SECTION 1. A new chapter to title 23 of the North Dakota Century Code is created and
9 enacted as follows:
10 Definitions
11 For purpose of this chapter:
12 1. "Commissioner" means the commissioner of the department.
13 2. "Department" means the department of health and human services.
14 Conditions for appointment of receiver.
15 1. When the department revokes the license of a mobile home park, the department may
16 file a petition with the district court to place the mobile home park under the control of
17 a receiver for repeated or serious violations of chapter 23 - 10 or the administrative
18 rules of the department, or to protect health or safety.
19 2. The court may grant the petition after finding:
20 a. The mobile home park committed, or is continuing to commit repeated or serious
21 violations of chapter 23 - 10 or the administrative rules of the department; or
22 b. Health or safety would be seriously threatened if a condition existing at the time
23 the petition was filed continues.
Page No. 1 25.1016.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 Appointment of receiver.
2 If the court grants the petition to place the mobile home park into receivership, the court
3 shall appoint the commissioner as receiver. The commissioner may designate a qualified
4 individual or a nonprofit organization to execute the receivership. An individual designated to
5 execute the receivership may not be employed by this state or a political subdivision. The
6 receiver shall use the income and assets of the mobile home park to maintain and operate the
7 mobile home park and to attempt to correct the violations of chapter 23 - 10, the administrative
8 rules of the department, or other condition which constitutes a threat to health or safety. The
9 receiver may not liquidate the assets of the mobile home park.
10 Termination of receivership.
11 The receivership terminates when:
12 1. The receiver and the court certify the conditions that prompted the receivership are
13 corrected;
14 2. The license to operate the mobile home park is restored;
15 3. A new license to operate a mobile home park is issued; or
16 4. The owner of the mobile home park discontinues operation and the residents of the
17 mobile home park have secured other appropriate housing.
18 Accounting.
19 Upon termination of the receivership, the receiver shall render a complete accounting to the
20 court and shall dispose of surplus funds as the court directs.

22 and enacted as follows:

27 telephone number for the mobile home park managers and any individual possessing
28 more than a twenty percent ownership interest in the entity subject to the license.
29 2. The department may assess a civil penalty not exceeding five thousand dollars for
30 each violation of this section thirty days after issuing a notice of noncompliance. The
31 civil penalty may be assessed without notice and a hearing. The civil penalty must be

3 23-10-12. Revocation or suspension of license - Penalty for operating without license.
4 1. The department may deny an application or take disciplinary action, up to and
5 including suspension or revocation of a license for a mobile home park, recreational
6 vehicle park, or campground, and revocation of a license for a recreational vehicle
7 park or campground, against any applicant or licensee upon the failure of the applicant

9 promulgated by the department.
10 2. Before the department takes disciplinary action against a license, the department shall
11 notify the licensee in writing of the reason disciplinary action is being considered and
12 shall provide a reasonable amount of time for correction to be made. Action taken
13 under the authority granted in this section must comply with chapter 28-32. Any person
14 who maintains or operates a mobile home park, recreational vehicle park, or
15 campground without first obtaining a license, or who operates the same during
16 suspension of the license, is guilty of an infraction a class B misdemeano r.
17 3. The department may assess a civil penalty of one hundred dollars per day against a
18 person who maintains or operates a mobile home park, recreational vehicle park, or
19 campground without first obtaining a license. The civil penalty may not exceed ten
20 thousand dollars. The proceeds of the civil penalty must be transferred to the
21 department's general operating fund.

23 and enacted as follows:
24 Mobile home park tenant relations.
25 1. A mobile home park owner shall provide a tenant with a summary outlining the
26 tenant's lease and the rights and obligations of the tenant and mobile home park
27 owner under state law. The department shall create forms for the administration of this
28 section.
29 2. A notice of intent to evict a tenant from a mobile home park under section 47 - 32 - 02
30 must include clear, bold face language stating:

5 daily late fee of more than five dollars per day.

21 that any change to how payments are applied is communicated clearly to
22 tenants.
23 i. Require mobile home park owners to provide tenants with clear instruction on the
24 acceptable methods to pay rent and any fees that accompany a method of
25 payment.
26 6. The department shall enforce this section and may assess a maximum civil penalty of
27 ten thousand dollars per violation. In addition to assessing a penalty, the department
28 may order the removal of lease terms that violate this section. A person assessed a
29 penalty under this section has the right to appeal the department's order under chapter
30 28 - 32 .
