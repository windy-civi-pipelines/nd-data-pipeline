1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact sections 43-13-01, 43-13-02, 43-13-05, 43-13-07,
2 43-13-08, 43-13-13, 43-13-13.2, 43-13-13.3, 43-13-15, 43-13-16, 43-13-17, and 43-13-19 of the
3 North Dakota Century Code, relating to optometrist licensure.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
5 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 43-13-01 of the North Dakota Century Code is
6 amended and reenacted as follows:
7 43-13-01. Definitions.
8 In this chapter, unless the context or subject matter otherwise requires:
9 1. "Board" means the North Dakota state board of optometry.
10 2. "Diagnosis and treatment" means the determination, interpretation, and treatment of
11 any visual, muscular, neurological, or anatomical anomaly of the eye which may be
12 aided, relieved, or corrected through visual training procedures or through the use of
13 lenses, prisms, filters, ophthalmic instruments, pharmaceutical agents, or
14 combinations thereof, held either in contact with the eye, or in frames or mountings, as
15 further authorized by this chapter. Laser therapy and the use of invasive surgery are
16 not permitted under this chapter, except superficial foreign bodies may be removed
17 and primary care procedures may be performed.
18 3. "Optometry" means a primary health care profession whose practitioners are engaged
19 in the evaluation of disorders of the human eye and the examination, diagnosis, and
20 treatment thereof, together with its appendagesDistant site provider" means a provider
21 of optometric services through telemedicine from a site other than the patient's
22 origination site. A distant site provider must hold an active license to practice
23 optometry in this state.
Page No. 1 25.0935.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 4. "Optometric services" means any evaluation, examination, diagnosis, or treatment
2 provided for a patient within the scope of practice of optometry. The term includes the
3 prescription of pharmaceutical agents by a provider to a patient.
4 5. "Pharmaceutical agent" means diagnostic pharmaceutical agents or therapeutic
5 pharmaceutical agents. The term includes nonscheduled pharmaceutical agents,
6 except for acetaminophen with thirty milligrams of codeine, that have documented use
7 in the treatment of ocular-related disorders or diseases. As used in this subsection:
8 a. "Diagnostic pharmaceutical agents" means pharmaceutical agents administered
9 for the evaluation and diagnosis of disorders of the human eye including
10 anesthetics, mydriatics, myotics, cycloplegics, diagnostic dyes, diagnostic stains,
11 and pharmaceutical agents to evaluate abnormal pupil responses.
12 b. "Therapeutic pharmaceutical agents" includes topically administered and
13 prescribed pharmaceutical agents for treatment of ocular-related disorders or
14 disease, locally administered pharmaceutical agents for primary eye care
15 procedures, oral anti-infective agents, oral antihistaminic agents, and oral
16 analgesics for the treatment of ocular-related disorders or diseases.
17 5. "Practicing optometry" means:
18 a. Displaying a sign or in any way advertising as an optometrist.
19 b. Employing any means for the measurement of the powers of vision or the
20 adaptation of lenses for the aid thereof.
21 c. Engaging in any manner in the practice of optometry
22 6. "Practice of optometry" means the examination of the human eye and its appendages,
23 and the employment of any means for the measurement of the powers of visions or
24 any visual, muscular, neurological, interpretative, or anatomical anomalies of the visual
25 processes, and the prescribing or employment of lenses, prism, frames, mountings,
26 visual training procedure, the prescribing or administration, except by injection of
27 pharmaceutical agents related to the diagnosis and treatment of the human eye and
28 its appendages, and any other means or method for correction, remedy, or relief of
29 any insufficiencies or abnormal conditions of the visual processes of the human eye
30 and its appendages, including removal of superficial foreign bodies from the eye,
31 except surgery.

12 3. Physicians and surgeons licensed under chapter 43-17.

13 1. To enforce the provisions and carry out the purposes of this chapter.

26 e. Endorsement fees;
27 f. Licensure verification fee;
28 g. Decorative wall certificate fee; and
29 h. Continuing education fees.

1 43-13-13.2. Practice of optometry - Certification requirements -
2 Notification Requirements to practice optometry - Informed consent .
3 1. Any person engaged in visual training procedures or who employs or prescribes
4 lenses, prisms, filters, ophthalmic instruments, or combinations thereof, held either in
5 contact with the eye, or in frames or mounting, to aid, relieve, or correct any visual or
6 ocular anomaly, or holds out as being able to do so, is deemed to be engaged in the
7 practice of optometry.
8 2. Before any optometrist may prescribe and administer pharmaceutical agents in the
9 treatment and management of ocular diseases, the optometrist must first be certified
10 or qualify for certification in the use of diagnostic pharmaceutical agents. For additional
11 certification to prescribe and administer pharmaceutical agents in the treatment and
12 management of ocular disease, the board shall require at least seventy-six hours of
13 didactic instruction and twenty-four hours of clinical application of pharmaceutical
14 agents for the treatment and management of ocular diseases. The course for
15 therapeutic certification must be provided by an institution accredited by a regional or
16 professional accrediting organization that is recognized and approved by the United
17 States department of education or the council on postsecondary accreditation.
18 3. An optometrist may not dispense therapeutic pharmaceutical agents, except an
19 optometrist may:
20 a. Provide a patient a drug sample at no cost to the patient; or
21 b. Sell contact lenses or ophthalmic devices that are classified by the federal food
22 and drug administration as a drug.
23 4.3. The board shall provide the board of pharmacy upon request a list of licensed
24 optometrists certified in the use of pharmaceutical agents.An optometric telemedicine
25 provider-patient relationship is established when a patient seeks optometric services
26 from a provider, and the provider agrees to provide optometric services to the patient,
27 except in an emergency. The provider-patient relationship may be expressly created or
28 created through implication; however, the provider-patient relationship is not created
29 through receipt of patient health information by the provider unless a prior provider-
30 patient relationship exists. The initial patient relationship must be established through

1 an eye examination conducted by a licensed optometrist with a physical location in this
2 state.
3 4. A licensed optometrist may use telemedicine to provide care in accordance with
4 standards of practice established by the board. Telemedicine services may include
5 consultation, diagnosis, and treatment for ocular diseases, provided that the
6 optometrist complies with the regulations under this section. Before providing any
7 optometric services to a patient via telemedicine, the provider first shall provide
8 appropriate verification of the provider's identification, licensure, and current contact
9 information.
10 5. Informed consent regarding the optometric services to be provided via telemedicine
11 must include, at a minimum, information regarding the provider's technology used to
12 provide optometric services via telemedicine and how to mitigate or resolve any
13 technological disconnection or issue.
14 SECTION 8. AMENDMENT. Section 43-13-13.3 of the North Dakota Century Code is
15 amended and reenacted as follows:
16 43-13-13.3. Standard of care - When consultation with physician required.
17 1. An optometrist certified by the board in the use of pharmaceutical agents as provided in
18 this chapter must be held to the same standard of care in the use of such agents as are
19 physicians licensed by the North Dakota board of medicine.
20 2. Any optometrist authorized by the board to use pharmaceutical agents shall consult
21 with a physician duly licensed to practice medicine when any diseased or pathological
22 conditions of the eye do not respond to treatment. The consultation must be
23 documented in the patient's record.
