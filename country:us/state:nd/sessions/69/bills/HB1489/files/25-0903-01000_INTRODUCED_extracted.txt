1 SECTION 11. Chapter 14-07.7 of the North Dakota Century Code is created and enacted
2 as follows:
3 14 - 07.7 - 01. Definitions .
4 For purposes of this chapter:
5 1. a. "Civil protection order" means a protection order that prohibits the restrained
6 individual from:
7 (1) Contacting, harassing, injuring, intimidating, molesting, threatening,
8 touching, stalking, sexually assaulting, or abusing any protected individual;
9 (2) Entering or remaining on premises;
10 (3) Coming within a specified distance of the protected individual or premises;
11 or
12 (4) Any other action necessary to protect the protected individual from imminent
13 danger to life or health.
14 b. A civil protection order may be a:
15 (1) Disorderly conduct restraining order;
16 (2) Domestic violence protection order; or
17 (3) Sexual assault restraining order.
18 2. "Contact" means any interaction or communication with another individual, directly or
19 indirectly, including electronic, digital, and social media communication.
20 3. "Disorderly conduct" means intrusive or unwanted acts, words, or gestures intended to
21 adversely affect the safety, security, or privacy of another individual. Disorderly
22 conduct includes human trafficking and attempted human trafficking as defined in
23 title 12.1. Disorderly conduct does not include constitutionally protected activity .
24 4. "Domestic violence" includes physical harm, bodily injury, sexual activity compelled by
25 physical force, assault, or the infliction of fear of imminent physical harm, bodily injury,
26 sexual activity compelled by physical force, or assault, not committed in self-defense,
27 on the complaining family or household member.
28 5. "Family or household member" means a spouse, family member, former spouse,
29 parent, child, individual related by blood or marriage, individuals in a dating
30 relationship, individuals residing together or who have resided together in the past,
31 individuals with a child in common regardless of relationship status and, for the

2 relationship to the abusing individual as determined by the court under section
3 14 - 07.7 - 07 .
4 6. "Protected individual" means the individual identified in a civil protection order as the
5 individual for whose benefit the civil protection order was issued.
6 7. "Sexual assault" means any nonconsensual offense in chapter 12.1-20 for which
7 sexual act or sexual conduct, as defined in section 12.1-20-02, is an element.
8 8. "Stalking" has the meaning as in section 12.1-17-07.1. Domestic violence and
9 disorderly conduct include stalking.
10 14 - 07.7 - 02. Petition for civil protection order .
11 1. An individual who is or has been a victim of disorderly conduct, domestic violence, or
12 sexual assault may file a petition for a civil protection order against:
13 a. A family or household member who commits an act of domestic violence; or
14 b. An individual who has committed disorderly conduct or sexual assault.
15 2. The petition must identify which type of civil protection order is sought.
16 3. If the individual to be protected is a minor, the parent or guardian shall file a petition on
17 behalf of the minor. The parent or guardian of the minor is the petitioner and the minor
18 is the protected individual.
19 4. If the respondent is a minor, the parent or guardian must be notified of the petition and
20 any subsequent order.
21 5. The petition must allege facts sufficient to show:
22 a. The name of the alleged victim;
23 b. The name of the respondent engaging in the alleged conduct; and
24 c. The respondent engaged in the alleged conduct.
25 6. The petition must contain:
26 a. A declaration stating the specific facts and circumstances supporting the relief
27 sought; and
28 b. A statement listing each civil or criminal action involving both parties.
29 7. A petition may be against only one respondent. Dual protection orders in a single
30 action are prohibited.
