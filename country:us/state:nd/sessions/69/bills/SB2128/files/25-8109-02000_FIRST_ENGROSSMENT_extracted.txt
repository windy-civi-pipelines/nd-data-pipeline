1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact sections 12-44.1-01, 12-47-18.1, 12-48.1-01,
2 12-48.1-02, 12-54.1-01, 12-54.1-03, 12.1-08-02, 12.1-17-01, 12.1-32-02.1, 12.1-32-09.1, and
3 39-10-71 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to transparent sentencing of criminal
4 offenders, transfer of persons between correctional facilities, work release eligibility and
5 conditions for criminal offenders, sentences for assaulting and fleeing from law enforcement
6 officers, and sentences for preventing arrest; to provide a legislative management report; and to
7 provide a penalty.
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
9 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 12-44.1-01 of the North Dakota Century Code is
10 amended and reenacted as follows:
11 12-44.1-01. Definitions.
12 As used in this chapter:
13 1. "Administrator" means the sheriff, chief of police, administrator, superintendent,
14 director, or other individual serving as the chief executive officer of a correctional
15 facility.
16 2. "Adult lockup" means a secure temporary-hold nonresidential facility that does not
17 hold individuals overnight and includes a facility with cuffing rails or cuffing benches.
18 3. "Correctional facility" means a city or county jail or detention center, regional
19 corrections center, or juvenile detention center for the detention or confinement of
20 persons in accordance with law. The use of the term does not imply and may not be
21 used to require the provision of services including treatment, counseling, career and
22 technical education, or other educational services, except as may otherwise be
Page No. 1 25.8109.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 required or provided for under this chapter. The term does not include transitional
2 facilities.
3 4. "Correctional facility staff" means correctional personnel with titles such as jailer,
4 deputy, counselor, correctional officer, or any other title, whose duties include the
5 ongoing supervision of inmates in a correctional facility.
6 5. "Court holding facility" means a secure facility, other than an adult correctional facility
7 or adult lockup, used to temporarily detain individuals before or after a detention
8 hearing or other court proceedings, and is not used to detain individuals overnight.
9 6. "Individual justice planning" means a process to identify, accommodate, and develop
10 appropriate consequences for behaviors caused by or related to an individual's mental
11 or cognitive impairment.
12 7. "Inmate" means any individual, whether sentenced or unsentenced, who is detained or
13 confined in a correctional facility. The term does not include an individual who is under
14 the supervision of the correctional facility and is supervised under home detention,
15 electronic monitoring, or a similar program that does not involve physical detention or
16 confinement in the facility.
17 8. "Jail" means a correctional facility, including a county or city jail or a regional
18 corrections center.
19 9. "Juvenile detention center" means a publicly maintained correctional facility for the
20 detention of juveniles. The term does not include the North Dakota youth correctional
21 center.
22 10. "Regional corrections center" means a correctional facility established and maintained
23 by more than one county or city, or a combination of counties and cities, for the
24 confinement of inmates.
25 11. "Trained correctional facility staff" means correctional personnel who have completed
26 a course of training approved by the peace officer standards and training board.
27 12. "Transitional facility" means an organization or facility, operating under contract, in
28 partnership with, or under the direction of the department of corrections and
29 rehabilitation or a correctional facility, which offers education, counseling, or other
30 programs, with minimum security housing, for offenders.

4 SECTION 4. AMENDMENT. Section 12-48.1-02 of the North Dakota Century Code is
5 amended and reenacted as follows:
6 12-48.1-02. Conditions of eligibility forparticipation in release programs.
7 1. An eligible offender, except an offender sentenced to a penalty of life imprisonment
8 without the opportunity for parole as the result of conviction of a class AA felony under
9 section 12.1-20-03 or of murder under section 12.1-16-01, may be eligible for
10 programsparticipate in a program outside facilitiesa facility under the control of the
11 department of corrections and rehabilitation when the department determines, with a
12 high degree of reliability, the eligible offender is not a high security risk, not likely to
13 commit a crime of violence, not likely to escape, and is likely to be rehabilitated by
14 such program. An eligible offender may apply to the director of the department for
15 permission to participate in such programs.
16 2. The director of the department may authorize participation in outside programs for an
17 eligible offender who has ten years or less remaining on a sentence and has been
18 committed to the legal and physical custody of the department. The parole board, with
19 the approval of the director of the department, may authorize participation in outside
20 programs for eligible offenders who have more than ten years remaining on a
21 sentence and have been committed to the legal and physical custody of the
22 department.
23 3. The offender shall submit a signed application which must include a statement that the
24 eligible offender agrees to abide by all terms and conditions of the particular plan
25 adopted for the eligible offender, and must include such other information as the
26 parole board or the director of the department may require.
27 4. The parole board may approve, disapprove, or defer action on an application
28 approved by the director of the department. The director of the department or the
29 parole board may revoke approval of the application at any time after granting the
30 application. The department shall prescribe rules of conduct and treatment for all
31 eligible offenders on release programs and shall prescribe objective and subjective

1 criteria for which revocation of approval to participate in release programs is
2 mandatory. A rule violation indicating the eligible offender likely will commit a crime of
3 violence or is likely to attempt to escape must result in revocation of approval to
4 participate in release programs. The department shall document all violations of the
5 rules of conduct and treatment.
6 5. The director of the department may grant short leaves, not to exceed seventy-two
7 hours, to eligible offenders who have been committed to the legal and physical
8 custody of the department for ten years or less. The parole board, upon the approval
9 of the director of the department, may grant short leaves, not to exceed seventy-two
10 hours, to offenders committed to the legal and physical custody of the department for
11 more than ten years. Short leaves granted under this subsection may not be granted
12 consecutively to the same individual.
13 6. All rules adopted by the parole board and the director of the department relating to
14 release programs and short leaves must conform, to the extent allowable by law, with
15 executive order no. 11755 issued by the President of the United States.

18 12-54.1-01. Sentence reduction for good time.
19 Except as provided underSubject to the requirements of section 12.1-32-09.1, an offender

21 is eligible to earn sentence reductions based upon satisfactory fulfillment of performance criteria
22 established through department and penitentiary rules. Performance criteria includesmust

24 and good work performance. The department may credit an offender committed to the legal and
25 physical custody of the department who is eligible for sentence reduction up to five days good
26 time per month for each month of the sentence imposedserved, up to a maximum of fifteen
27 percent of the offender's sentence. Sentence reductions may not be awarded before they are
28 earned. The department may credit an offender with sentence reduction for time spent in
29 custody before sentencing and commitment to the legal and physical custody of the
30 department. The department may not credit an offender with any sentence reduction for time
31 spent on probation under the supervision and management of the department.

15 amended and reenacted as follows:
16 12.1-08-02. Preventing arrest or discharge of other duties.
17 1. A person is guilty of a class A misdemeanor if, with intent to prevent a public servant
18 from effecting an arrest of himself or another for a misdemeanor or infraction, or from
19 discharging any other official duty, he creates a substantial risk of bodily injury to the public
20 servant or to anyone except himself, or employs means justifying or requiring substantial force
21 to overcome resistance to effecting the arrest or the discharge of the duty. A person is guilty of a
22 class C felony if, with intent to prevent a public servant from effecting an arrest of himself or
23 another for a class A, B, or C felony, he creates a substantial risk of bodily injury to the public
24 servant or to anyone except himself, or employs means justifying or requiring substantial force
25 to overcome resistance to effecting such an arrest.
26 2.1. It is a defense to a prosecution under this section that the public servant was not
27 acting lawfully, but it is no defense that the defendant mistakenly believed that the
28 public servant was not acting lawfully. A public servant executing a warrant or other
29 process in good faith and under color of law shall be deemed to be acting lawfully.

4 SECTION 8. AMENDMENT. Section 12.1-17-01 of the North Dakota Century Code is
5 amended and reenacted as follows:
6 12.1-17-01. Simple assault.
7 1. A person is guilty of an offense if that person:
8 a. Willfully causes bodily injury to another human being; or
9 b. Negligently causes bodily injury to another human being by means of a firearm,
10 destructive device, or other weapon, the use of which against a human being is
11 likely to cause death or serious bodily injury.
12 2. The offense is:
13 a. A class C felony when the victim is a peace officer or correctional institution
14 employee acting in an official capacity, which the actor knows to be a fact; an
15 employee of the state hospital acting in the course and scope of employment,
16 which the actor knows to be a fact, and the actor is an individual committed to or
17 detained at the state hospital pursuant to chapter 25-03.3; a person engaged in a
18 judicial proceeding; or a member of a municipal or volunteer fire department or
19 emergency medical services personnel unit or emergency department worker in
20 the performance of the member's duties.
21 b. A class B misdemeanor except as provided in subdivision a.
22 c. A conviction under subdivision a carries a penalty of at least thirty days'
23 imprisonment and, if there is an underlying conviction, the imprisonment must be
24 consecutive to any sentence of imprisonment for the underlying conviction.

1 SECTION 11. AMENDMENT. Section 39-10-71 of the North Dakota Century Code is
2 amended and reenacted as follows:
3 39-10-71. Fleeing or attempting to elude a peace officer - Penalty.
4 1. A driver of a motor vehicle who willfully fails or refuses to bring the vehicle to a stop, or
5 who otherwise flees or attempts to elude, in any manner, a pursuing police vehicle or
6 peace officer, when given a visual or audible signal to bring the vehicle to a stop, is
7 guilty of a:
8 a. Class A misdemeanor for a first offense and a class C felony for a subsequent
9 offense within three years;
10 b. Class C felony if the driver violates this section while willfully fleeing during or
11 after the commission of a felony; or
12 c. Class C felony if, at any time during the flight or pursuit, the driver willfully
13 operates the vehicle in a manner constituting an inherent risk of death or serious
14 bodily injury to a third person.
15 2. A signal complies with this section if the signal is perceptible to the driver and:
16 a. If given from a vehicle, the signal is given by hand, voice, emergency light, or
17 siren, and the stopping vehicle is appropriately marked showing it to be an official
18 police vehicle; or
19 b. If not given from a vehicle, the signal is given by hand, voice, emergency light, or
20 siren, and the officer is in uniform or prominently displays the officer's badge of
21 office.
22 3. A felony conviction under this section carries a penalty of at least thirty days'
23 imprisonment and, if there is an underlying conviction, the imprisonment must be
24 consecutive to any sentence of imprisonment for the underlying conviction.
25 4. Upon a motion by a state's attorney, a court may order that a motor vehicle used in the
26 commission of a violation of this section be impounded by a peace officer for up to six
27 months.
