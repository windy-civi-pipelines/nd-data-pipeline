Section 1:
25.1279.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
with Senate Amendments
Introduced by
Representatives D. Anderson, Headland, O'Brien, Schauer, Vollmer, Novak
Senators Dwyer, Thomas
1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact section 54-17.8-06 of the North Dakota Century Code,
2 relating to the North Dakota outdoor heritage advisory board; and to declare an emergency.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
4 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 54-17.8-06 of the North Dakota Century Code is
5 amended and reenacted as follows:
6 54-17.8-06. North Dakota outdoor heritage advisory board - Members.
7 1. There is created a North Dakota outdoor heritage advisory board consisting of twelve
8 members. The governor shall appoint representatives from each of the groups listed in
9 this section based upon recommendations made by the appropriate group. The
10 advisory board consists of:
11 a. Four members from the agriculture community. The governor shall appoint one
12 member from the North Dakota farm bureau, North Dakota farmers union, the
13 North Dakota stockmen's association, and the North Dakota grain growers
14 association.
15 b. Two members from the energy industry. The governor shall appoint one member
16 from the North Dakota petroleum council and one member from the lignite energy
17 council.
18 c. Four members from the conservation community. The governor shall appoint the
19 members from the conservation community at large of statewide conservation
20 groups.
21 d. One member from the business community from the greater North Dakota
22 chamber.
Page No. 1 25.1279.03000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 e. One member from the North Dakota recreation and park association.
2 2. The governor also shall appoint to the advisory board one representative from each of
3 the following agencies to serve as ex officio, nonvoting technical members: the
4 department of parks and recreation, the department of water resources, the game and
5 fish department, the office of the state forester, and the North Dakota association of
6 soil conservation districts.
7 3. The term of office of each member of the board is four years and members may not
8 serve more than two consecutive terms. The terms of office commence on the first day
9 of July. The initial terms for the advisory board members must be staggered following
10 a method determined by the board.
11 4. The advisory board shall select a chairman from among the members. Seven voting
12 members is a quorum at any meeting.
13 5. The advisory board shall have at least two regular meetings each year and additional
14 meetings as the chairman determines necessary at a time and place to be fixed by the
15 chairman. Special meetings must be called by the chairman on written request of any
16 five members.
17 6. The advisory board may not forward a grant application to the commission unless the
18 application is for funding activities that fulfill the purposes of this chapter and the
19 application receives a favorable recommendation from a majority of the advisory board
20 members present at a meeting at which a quorum is present.
21 7. Members of the advisory board appointed by the governor serve at the pleasure of the
22 governor.
23 SECTION 2. EMERGENCY. This Act is declared to be an emergency measure.
