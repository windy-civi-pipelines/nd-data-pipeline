Section 1:
25.1279.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
Introduced by
Representatives D. Anderson, Headland, O'Brien, Schauer, Vollmer, Novak
Senators Dwyer, Thomas
1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact section 54-17.8-06 of the North Dakota Century Code,
2 relating to the North Dakota outdoor heritage advisory board.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
4 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 54-17.8-06 of the North Dakota Century Code is
5 amended and reenacted as follows:
6 54-17.8-06. North Dakota outdoor heritage advisory board - Members.
7 1. There is created a North Dakota outdoor heritage advisory board consisting of twelve
8 members. The governor shall appoint representatives from each of the groups listed in
9 this section based upon recommendations made by the appropriate group. The
10 advisory board consists of:
11 a. Four members from the agriculture community. The governor shall appoint one
12 member from the North Dakota farm bureau, North Dakota farmers union, the
13 North Dakota stockmen's association, and the North Dakota grain growers
14 association.
15 b. Two members from the energy industry. The governor shall appoint one member
16 from the North Dakota petroleum council and one member from the lignite energy
17 council.
18 c. Four members from the conservation community. The governor shall appoint the
19 members from the conservation community at large of statewide conservation
20 groups.
21 d. One member from the business community from the greater North Dakota
22 chamber.
23 e. One member from the North Dakota recreation and park association.
Page No. 1 25.1279.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 2. The governor also shall appoint to the advisory board one representative from each of
2 the following agencies to serve as ex officio, nonvoting technical members: the
3 department of parks and recreation, the department of water resources, the game and
4 fish department, the office of the state forester, and the North Dakota association of
5 soil conservation districts.
6 3. The term of office of each member of the board is four years and members may not
7 serve more than two consecutive terms. The terms of office commence on the first day
8 of July. The initial terms for the advisory board members must be staggered following
9 a method determined by the board.
10 4. The advisory board shall select a chairman from among the members. Seven voting
11 members is a quorum at any meeting.
12 5. The advisory board shall have at least two regular meetings each year and additional
13 meetings as the chairman determines necessary at a time and place to be fixed by the
14 chairman. Special meetings must be called by the chairman on written request of any
15 five members.
16 6. The advisory board may not forward a grant applicationshall recommend to the
17 commission unless the application isapproval of grants for funding activities that fulfill
18 the purposes of this chapter and the application receives a favorable recommendation
19 from a majority of the advisory board members.
20 7. Members of the advisory board appointed by the governor serve at the pleasure of the
21 governor.
