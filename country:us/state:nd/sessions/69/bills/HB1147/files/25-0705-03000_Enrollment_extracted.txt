Sixty-ninth Legislative Assembly of North Dakota
HOUSE BILL NO. 1147
(Representatives D. Johnston, Brandenburg, Kasper, Marschall, Nehring, M. Ruby, Tveit)
(Senators Clemens, Paulson)
AN ACT to amend and reenact section 20.1-03-12 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to fees
for game and fish licenses and permits.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:

Section 2:
SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 20.1-03-12 of the North Dakota Century Code is amended and
reenacted as follows:

Section 3:
20.1-03-12. Schedule of fees for licenses and permits.
The fees collected under this section for licenses and permits must be deposited with the state
treasurer and credited to the game and fish fund, except aquatic nuisance species fees must be
deposited with the state treasurer and credited to the aquatic nuisance species program fund. Forty-five
dollars of each nonresident big game hunting license fee must be used for the private land initiative.
The various license and permit fees are as follows:

Section 4:
1. For a resident, age sixteen and over, small game hunting license, ten dollars.

Section 5:
2. For a nonresident small game hunting license, one hundred dollars.

Section 6:
3. For a resident big game hunting license, thirty dollars, except the fee for a licensee under age
sixteen is ten dollars, except as provided in a gubernatorial proclamation issued pursuant to

Section 7:
section 20.1-08-04.1.

Section 8:

Section 9:

nonresident bow license, two hundred fifty dollars, and a nonrefundable five dollar application
fee must accompany any lottery license fee under this subsection, except as provided in a
gubernatorial proclamation issued pursuant to section 20.1-08-04.1. For a nonresident who
participates on the same basis as a resident in a lottery for deer licenses remaining after the
second lottery for residents, fifty dollars.

Section 10:
5. For a resident fur-bearer license, fifteen dollars.

Section 11:
6. For a resident fishing license, sixteen dollars, except that for a resident sixty-five years or over,
a resident totally or permanently disabled, or a resident disabled veteran who has at least a
fifty percent service-connected disability as determined by the department of veterans' affairs
or has an extra-schedular rating to include individual unemployability that brings the veteran's
total disability ratio to at least fifty percent, the license fee is five dollars.

[.40 hectare] for each acre [.40 hectare].

Section 35:
30. For a nonresident waterfowl hunting license, one hundred dollars.

Section 36:
31. For a nonresident husband and wife fishing license, sixty dollars.

32. For a nonresident short-term three-day fishing license, twenty-five dollars.

Section 61:
56. For each motorboat operated on waters of this state and not licensed in this state, an aquatic
