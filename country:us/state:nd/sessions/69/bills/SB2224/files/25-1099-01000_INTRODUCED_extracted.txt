Section 1:
25.1099.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
Introduced by
Senators Myrdal, Luick
1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact section 53-06.1-01.1 of the North Dakota Century
2 Code, relating to the abolition of the gaming commission and the authorization of the attorney
3 general to administer and regulate gaming.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
5 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 53-06.1-01.1 of the North Dakota Century Code is
6 amended and reenacted as follows:
7 53-06.1-01.1. Gaming commissionAttorney general - Gaming - Rules.
8 1. The state gaming commission consists of the chairman and four other members
9 appointed by the governor, with the consent of the senate. The members serve
10 three-year terms and until a successor is appointed and qualified. If the senate is not
11 in session when the term of a member expires, the governor may make an interim
12 appointment, and the interim appointee holds office until the senate confirms or rejects
13 the appointment. A member appointed to fill a vacancy arising from other than the
14 natural expiration of a term serves only for the unexpired portion of the term. The
15 terms of the commissioners must be staggered so no more than two terms expire
16 each July first.
17 2. A person is ineligible for appointment to the commission if that person has not been a
18 resident of this state for at least two years before the date of appointment. A person is
19 also ineligible if that person is not of such character and reputation as to promote
20 public confidence in the administration of gaming in this state. A person is also
21 ineligible if that person has been convicted of a felony criminal offense or has pled
22 guilty or been found guilty of any violation of chapter 12.1-06, 12.1-08, 12.1-09,
23 12.1-10, 12.1-11, 12.1-12, 12.1-22, 12.1-23, 12.1-24, 12.1-28, 53-06.1, or 53-06.2, or
24 has pled guilty or been found guilty of any violation of section 6-08-16 or 6-08-16.2, or
Page No. 1 25.1099.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 has pled guilty or been found guilty of any offense or violation that has a direct bearing
2 on the person's fitness to be involved in gaming, or who has committed an equivalent
3 offense or violation of the laws of another state or of the United States. A person who
4 has a financial interest in gaming or is an employee or a member of the gaming
5 committee of a licensed organization or distributor cannot be a member of the
6 commission. For the purpose of this subsection, a financial interest includes the
7 receiving of any direct payment from an eligible organization for property, services, or
8 facilities provided to that organization.
9 3. Commission members are entitled to seventy-five dollars per day for compensation for
10 each day spent on commission duties and mileage and expense reimbursement as
11 allowed to other state employees.
12 4. The commissionattorney general shall adopt rules in accordance with chapter 28-32, to
13 administer and regulate the gaming industry, including methods:
14 1. Methods of conduct, play, and promotion of games; minimum
15 2. Minimum procedures and standards for recordkeeping and internal control; requiring
16 3. Requiring tax returns and reports from organizations or distributors; methods
17 4. Methods of competition and doing business by distributors and manufacturers;
18 acquisition
19 5. Acquisition and use of gaming equipment; quality
20 6. Quality standards or specifications for the manufacture of pull tabs, paper bingo cards,
21 electronic pull tab devices, pull tab dispensing devices, bingo card marking devices,
22 and fifty-fifty raffle systems; to
23 7. To ensure that net proceeds are used for educational, charitable, patriotic, fraternal,
24 religious, or public-spirited uses; to
25 8. To protect and promote the public interest; to
26 9. To ensure fair and honest games; to
27 10. To ensure that fees and taxes are paid; and to
28 11. To prevent and detect unlawful gambling activity.
