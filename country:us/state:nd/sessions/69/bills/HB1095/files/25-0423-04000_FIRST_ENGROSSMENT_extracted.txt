25.0423.04000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
Introduced by
Representatives Kiefert, Dobervich, Frelich, Karls, McLeod, Mitskog, S. Olson
Senator Lee
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new section to chapter 15.1-07 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to child protective services liaisons in school districts.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
4 SECTION 1. A new section to chapter 15.1-07 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
5 and enacted as follows:
6 Child protective services liaison - Duties - Training - Report.
7 1. As used in this section, "child protective services" means an "authorized agent" or the
8 "department" as the terms are defined under section 50 - 25.1 - 02 .
9 2. A school district may designate at least one child protective services liaison by
10 assigning the duties under subsection 3 to an individual hired for the position of child
11 protective services liaison.
12 3. A child protective services liaison shall:
13 a. Act as a point of contact for child protective services ;
14 b. Communicate with child protective services on behalf of the school district as
15 allowed by law;
16 c. Assist child protective services with a student case investigation as allowed by
17 law;
18 d. Assist with coordinating resources and support services for a student in the care
19 of child protective services;
20 e. Inform school personnel of a student's needs resulting from the student's
21 involvement with child protective services as necessary; and
22 f. Annually provide to the school district a report, including:
Page No. 1 25.0423.04000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 (1) The number of cases on which the liaison worked;
2 (2) Challenges resulting from collaboration with child protective services; and
3 (3) Recommendations for improving the partnership between child protective
4 services and the school district.
5 4. Child protective services shall provide:
6 a. Training to each child protective services liaison, within six months of the liaison's
7 designation, including:
8 (1) Identification of signs of child abuse and neglect;
9 (2) Procedures for reporting suspected child abuse and neglect;
10 (3) Child protective services protocols and procedures;
11 (4) Best practices for collaborating with child protective services to support
12 students; and
13 (5) Confidentiality and ethical considerations regarding student cases.
14 b. Ongoing training and professional development.
15 c. Assistance with internal school district investigations that may occur concurrently
16 with child protective service investigations.
