Section 1:
25.8070.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
Introduced by
Education Committee
(At the request of the State Board of Higher Education)
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new section to chapter 15-18.1 of the North Dakota
2 Century Code, relating to the national council for state authorization reciprocity agreements
3 membership; to amend and reenact sections 15-18.1-01, 15-18.1-02, 15-18.1-05, 15-18.1-06,
4 and 15-18.1-10, and subsection 4 of section 15-18.1-13 of the North Dakota Century Code,
5 relating to postsecondary education institution standards; and to repeal section 15-18.1-03 of
6 the North Dakota Century Code, relating to the authorization of a postsecondary educational
7 institution not operating in this state.
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
9 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 15-18.1-01 of the North Dakota Century Code is
10 amended and reenacted as follows:
11 15-18.1-01. Definitions.
12 As used in this chapter:
13 1. "Accredited" means approved by an accrediting body recognized by the United States
14 department of education.
15 2. "Authorization to operate" or like term means approval of the board or North Dakota
16 university system office, as applicable, to operate or to contract to operate a
17 postsecondary educational institution in this state.
18 2.3. "Board" means the state board of higher education.
19 4. "Distance education" is education using one or more types of technology to deliver
20 instruction to a student who is separated from the instructor and to support regular and
21 substantive interaction between the student and the instructor synchronously or
22 asynchronously.
Page No. 1 25.8070.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 3.5. "Education" or "educational services" or like term includes any class, course, or
2 program of training, instruction, or study.
3 4.6. "Educational credentials" means degrees, diplomas, certificates, transcripts, reports,
4 documents, or letters of designation, marks, appellations, series of letters, numbers, or
5 words which signify, purport, or are generally taken to signify enrollment, attendance,
6 progress, or satisfactory completion of the requirements or prerequisites for education
7 at a postsecondary educational institution operating in this statea postsecondary
8 credential leading to a diploma; certificate; or an associate, bachelor, master, or
9 doctorate degree. This term does not include a postsecondary credential leading to an
10 honorary degree or other unearned degree.
11 7. "Person" means an individual, organization, government, political subdivision, or
12 government agency or instrumentality. The term does not include environmental
13 elements, artificial intelligence, an animal, or an inanimate object.
14 5.8. "Physical presence" means a presence established by:
15 a. Maintaining a physical location in the state where students receive instruction or
16 providing instruction to students at a designated location and time in the state;
17 b. Maintaining an administrative office in the state for students receiving in-person
18 instruction or distance education;
19 c. Maintaining a mailing address in the state and a local telephone number;
20 d. Providing office space in the state for instructional or noninstructional staff; and
21 e. Providing student support services at a physical location in the state, whether
22 operated by or on behalf of the institution.
23 9. "Postsecondary educational institution" means an academic, technical, home study,
24 business, professional or other school, college, or university, or other person,
25 operating in this state, offering educational credentials or offering instruction or
26 educational services, primarily to any individual who has completed or terminated
27 secondary education or who is beyond the age of compulsory high school attendance,
28 for attainment of educational, or professional objectives at the associate in arts level or
29 higheragency or political subdivision of the state, individual, partnership, company,
30 firm, society, trust, association, corporation, or any combination that:
31 a. Is, owns, or operates a postsecondary educational institution.
Page No. 2 25.8070.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 b. Provides a postsecondary course of instruction leading to a degree.
2 c. Uses in its name the term "college", "academy", "institute", or "university" or a
3 similar term to imply the person is primarily engaged in the education of students
4 at the postsecondary level, and charges for its services.
5 6.10. "To grant" includes awarding, selling, conferring, bestowing, or giving.
6 7.11. "To offer" includes, in addition to its usual meanings, advertising, publicizing, soliciting,
7 or encouraging any individual, directly or indirectly, in any form, to perform the act
8 described.
9 8. "To operate" an educational institution, or like term, means to establish, keep, or
10 maintain any facility or location in this state where, from, or through which, education
11 is offered or given, or educational credentials are offered or granted, and includes
12 contracting with any person to perform any such act.
13 SECTION 2. AMENDMENT. Section 15-18.1-02 of the North Dakota Century Code is
14 amended and reenacted as follows:
15 15-18.1-02. Exemptions.
16 The following education and educational schools or institutions are exempted from the
17 provisions of this chapter:
18 1. Education sponsored by a bona fideEducational programs conducted by a trade,
19 business, professional, or fraternal organization, so recognized by the board, solely for
20 that organization's membership, or offered on a no-fee basis.
21 2. Education offered by charitable institutions, organizations, or agencies, so recognized
22 by the board, provided the education is not advertised or promoted as leading toward
23 educational credentials.
24 3. Postsecondary educational institutions established, operated, and governed by this or
25 any other state or its political subdivisions, as determined by the board and any
26 educational consortium that includes one or more of the institutions.
27 4. Private four-year institutions chartered or incorporated and operating in the state prior
28 to July 1, 1977, so long as the institutions retain accreditation by national or regional
29 accrediting agenciesand are recognized by the United States officedepartment of
30 education.
31 5. Schools of nursing regulated under chapter 43-12.1.
Page No. 3 25.8070.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 6. Native American colleges operating in this state, established by federally recognized
2 Indian tribes.
3 7. Postsecondary educational institutions not operating in this state.
4 8.7. Institutions whose only physical presence in this state consists of students enrolled in
5 practicums, internships, clinicals, or student teaching in this state.
6 9.8. Postsecondary career schools regulated under chapter 15-20.4.
7 SECTION 3. AMENDMENT. Section 15-18.1-05 of the North Dakota Century Code is
8 amended and reenacted as follows:
9 15-18.1-05. Minimum standards to operate with a physical presence in North Dakota.
10 All postsecondary educational institutions with a physical presence in the state must be
11 accredited by national or regional accrediting agencies recognized by the United States
12 department of education, have a federal financial responsibility composite score greater than
13 one and one - half, and provide students with institutional public disclosures as defined by the
14 board. The board additionally may additionally require such further evidence and make such
15 further investigation as in its judgment may be necessary. Any postsecondary educational
16 institution operating in this state seeking its first authorization to operate may be issued a
17 provisional authorization to operate on an annual basis until the institution becomes eligible for
18 accreditation by a recognized accrediting agency. Institutions issued a provisional authorization
19 to operate must demonstrate a substantial good-faith showing of progress toward such status.
20 Only uponUpon accreditation may an institution may become eligible for a regularstate
21 authorization to operate. An institution shall give written notification to the board within thirty
22 days of any change to the institution's accreditation status, change of ownership, or change in
23 federal financial responsibility composite score.
24 SECTION 4. AMENDMENT. Section 15-18.1-06 of the North Dakota Century Code is
25 amended and reenacted as follows:
26 15-18.1-06. Prohibition - Penalty.
27 1. A person may not:
28 a. Operate, in this state, a postsecondary educational institution not exempted from
29 the provisions of this chapter, unless the institution has a currently valid
30 authorization to operate issued under this chapter.
Page No. 4 25.8070.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 b. Instruct or educate, or offer to instruct or educate, including advertising or
2 soliciting for such purpose, enroll or offer to enroll, contract or offer to contract
3 with any person for such purpose, or award any educational credential, or
4 contract with any institution or party to perform any such act, at a facility or
5 location in this state unless suchthe person, group, or entity observes and is in
6 compliancecomplies with the minimum standards and criteria established by the
7 board and the policies adopted by the board.
8 c. Use the term "university", "institute", or "college" without authorization to do so
9 from the board.
10 d. Grant, or offer to grant, educational credentials, without authorization to do so
11 from the board.
12 2. A person who violates this section, or who fails or refuses to deposit with the board the
13 records required by the board under this chapter, is subject to a civil penalty not to
14 exceed one hundredone thousand five hundred dollars for each violation. Each day's
15 failure to comply with these provisions is a separate violation. The fine may be
16 imposed by the board in an administrative proceeding or by any court of competent
17 jurisdictionconsistent with board policy and procedure.
18 3. A person who willfully violates this section, or who willfully fails or refuses to deposit
19 with the board the records required by the board under this chapter, is guilty of a
20 class B misdemeanor. The criminal sanctions may be imposed by a court of
21 competent jurisdiction in an action brought by the attorney general of this state or a
22 state's attorney pursuant to section 15-18.1-12.
23 4. The board may suspend or revoke a person's authorization to offer distance education
24 to North Dakota residents until the person complies with the national council for state
25 authorization reciprocity agreements membership requirements, unless the person is
26 exempt under section 7 of this Act .
27 5. The North Dakota university system office will notify in writing a person found to be out
28 of compliance and the person will have thirty days to remedy the deficiency. If the
29 deficiency is not remedied within the thirty-day period, penalties may be assessed
30 under subsection 2 .

Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 SECTION 5. AMENDMENT. Section 15-18.1-10 of the North Dakota Century Code is
2 amended and reenacted as follows:
3 15-18.1-10. Board review.
4 A person aggrieved by a decision of the boardNorth Dakota university system respecting
5 denial or revocation of an authorization to operate, or the placing of conditions thereon, whether
6 on initial application or on application for renewal may appeal to the board in the manner
7 provided by the board. Appeals must be filed with the North Dakota university system within
8 thirty days of receiving the notice of noncompliance. The board shall review the appeal and
9 issue a final decision.
10 SECTION 6. AMENDMENT. Subsection 4 of section 15-18.1-13 of the North Dakota
11 Century Code is amended and reenacted as follows:
12 4. As used in this section, "duly authorized institution of higher learning" means an
13 institution that:
14 a. Has accreditation recognized by the United States secretarydepartment of
15 education or has the foreign equivalent of such accreditation;
16 b. Has an authorization to operate under this chapter or under chapter 15-20.4;
17 c. Operates in this state, and is exempt from this chapter under section 15-18.1-02
18 or is exempt from chapter 15-20.4 under section 15-20.4-02;
19 d. Does not operate in this state and is:
20 (1) Licensed by the appropriate state agency; and
21 (2) An active applicant for accreditation by an accrediting body recognized by
22 the United States secretarydepartment of education; or
23 e. Has been found by the state board of higher education or the state board for
24 career and technical education to meet standards of academic quality
25 comparable to those of an institution located in the United States that has
26 accreditation recognized by the United States secretarydepartment of education
27 to offer degrees of the type and level claimed.
28 SECTION 7. A new section to chapter 15-18.1 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
29 and enacted as follows:
Page No. 6 25.8070.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 Required national council for state authorization reciprocity agreements
2 membership - Exemption .
3 1. A postsecondary educational institution offering distance education programs to
4 students in North Dakota must be a member of the national council for state
5 authorization reciprocity agreements and comply with the requirements of the council.
6 Institutions must adhere to all standards, policies, and fees established by the national
7 council for state authorization reciprocity agreements for membership and
8 participation, including reporting requirements, quality standards, and consumer
9 protection measures.
10 2. A postsecondary educational institution that is ineligible to participate in national
11 council for state authorization reciprocity agreements due to composite score
12 requirements is exempt from the requirements under subsection 1 if the
13 postsecondary educational institution:
14 a. Is an eligible participant in student financial aid programs under title IV of the
15 Higher Education Act of 1965 [Pub . L. 89 - 329; 79 Stat. 1219; 20 U.S.C. 1001
16 e t seq.]; and
17 b. At the institution level, has a financial responsibility score calculated by an
18 independent auditor using the methodology prescribed by the United States
19 department of education greater than one and one - half .
20 3. The state board of higher education shall adopt a policy to implement this section,
21 which may include a fee.
22 SECTION 8. REPEAL. Section 15-18.1-03 of the North Dakota Century Code is repealed.
