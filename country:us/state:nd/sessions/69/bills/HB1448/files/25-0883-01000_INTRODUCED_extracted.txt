Section 1:
25.0883.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
Introduced by
Representatives Christy, Bahl, Hagert, Ista, Nathe, O'Brien, Toman, Warrey, Dockter
Senators Boschee, Meyer, Davison
1 A BILL for an Act to create and enact a new chapter to title 54 of the North Dakota Century
2 Code, relating to the advanced technology review committee, advanced technology grant
3 program, and advanced technology grant fund; to provide for a transfer; and to provide an
4 appropriation.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
6 SECTION 1. A new chapter to title 54 of the North Dakota Century Code is created and
7 enacted as follows:
8 Definitions.
9 In this chapter, unless the context otherwise requires:
10 1. "Chief information officer" means the chief information officer of the information
11 technology department.
12 2. "Commissioner" means the commissioner of the department of commerce.
13 3. "Committee" means the advanced technology review committee.
14 4. "Department" means the department of commerce.
15 5. "Vice chancellor for information technology" means the chief information officer and
16 vice chancellor for information technology of the North Dakota university system.
17 Advanced technology review committee - Membership - Meetings.
18 1. The advanced technology review committee consists of:
19 a. The chief information officer or a designee within the information technology
20 department;
21 b. The vice chancellor for information technology or a designee within the North
22 Dakota university system;
23 c. One individual from the private sector with significant information and advanced
24 technology knowledge, appointed by the governor;
Page No. 1 25.0883.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 d. One individual from the private sector with significant information and advanced
2 technology knowledge, appointed by the majority leader of the house of
3 representatives;
4 e. One individual from the private sector with significant information and advanced
5 technology knowledge, appointed by the majority leader of the senate; and
6 f. The commissioner, or a designee within the department of commerce, who shall
7 serve as a nonvoting member of the committee and provide recommendations to
8 the committee regarding grant awards.
9 2. By July first of each odd - numbered year, the chief information officer and vice
10 chancellor for information technology shall compile a list of individuals with significant
11 private sector information and advanced technology knowledge working within the
12 state to be forwarded to the governor and the majority leaders of the house of
13 representatives and senate. The governor and the majority leaders of the house of
14 representatives and senate must consider the listed individuals for committee
15 membership appointments.
16 3. The chief information officer, vice chancellor for information technology, and
17 commissioner are permanent members of the committee. The term of office of the
18 members of the committee appointed from the private sector is four years except the
19 initial term of office of the individual appointed by the majority leader of the senate is
20 three years and the initial term of office of the individual appointed by the governor is
21 two years. Each term of office commences on August first. Members serve at the
22 pleasure of the appointing entity and may be reappointed for additional terms. By
23 August first of each year, the chairman of the legislative management shall select one
24 of the appointed members to serve as the chairman of the committee. The chairman of
25 the committee shall select a vice chair. If a committee member ceases to qualify as a
26 member of the appointing entity, that individual's membership on the committee
27 ceases immediately and the appointing entity shall appoint a new member to the
28 committee for the remainder of the term.
29 4. A committee member representing the private sector is entitled to receive
30 compensation in an amount not exceeding one hundred thirty - five dollars per day and
31 travel and expense reimbursement as provided by law for state officers for attending
Page No. 2 25.0883.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 meetings of the committee, to be paid by the department within the limits of legislative
2 appropriations.
3 5. The committee shall meet as necessary at the call of the committee chairman to
4 review and approve grant applications from entrepreneurs, startup companies, and
5 small businesses that are in the initial phases of advanced technology product
6 research, development, and innovation and are unable to access financial resources
7 for prototype product development.
8 Advanced technology grant program - Eligibility - Use of funds.
9 1. The department shall administer the advanced technology grant program to provide
10 grants for activities identified in this chapter. The department shall provide
11 administrative support for the program, including the drafting of application forms,
12 receiving applications, reviewing applications for completeness and compliance with
13 committee policy, and forwarding complete applications to the committee in
14 accordance with the guidelines established by the committee.
15 2. The committee shall establish guidelines for entities to qualify for an advanced
16 technology grant under this section. Program guidelines relating to ownership of
17 intellectual property, inventions, and discoveries must address activities and issues
18 unique to technologies, patents, and companies created as a result of an advanced
19 technology grant.
20 3. In determining whether to approve an application for grant funding, the committee
21 shall consider the extent to which the applicant's proposal will support early stage
22 research and development of advance technologies, promote economic development,
23 and advance the workforce in the state. The committee shall give priority to
24 applications promoting the advancement of artificial intelligence, machine learning,
25 quantum computing, and similar research and development and product innovation
26 proposals. The committee shall consider and process applications in a timely manner
27 to allow applicants an opportunity to leverage other funds.
28 4. Grant recipients shall use funding awarded under this section to enhance advance
29 technology product research, development, and innovation, and to the extent possible,
30 leverage state, federal, and private sources of funding. An entity receiving a grant
31 under this section may not use the funds for capital or building investments or other
Page No. 3 25.0883.01000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 activities not identified in this chapter. The funds may not be used for administrative
2 costs or to supplant funding for regular operations of the entity. If an entity awarded a
3 grant no longer conducts its activities in the state, the entity must repay the awarded
4 grant funding to department.
5 Advanced technology grant fund.
6 The advanced technology grant fund is a special fund in the state treasury administered by
7 the department. Moneys in the fund must be used for providing grants and for the payment of
8 committee and department administrative expenses incurred related to the requirements of this
9 chapter, subject to legislative appropriations. Interest earned on moneys in the fund must be
10 credited to the fund.
11 Advanced technology grant program - Postaward monitoring.
12 Upon completion of work performed from funding provided by an advanced technology
13 grant, the department shall conduct an independent review of the results. Evaluation criteria
14 may include how the work performed has:
15 1. Contributed to development of an advanced technology product or process and
16 enhanced the ability of a company to make investments in the state, or otherwise
17 enticed a company to invest in or move to the state;
18 2. Promoted economic development of the public or private sectors in the state;
19 3. Led to a patent or research that is commercially viable; or
20 4. Positively affected workforce in the state.
21 SECTION 2. TRANSFER - STRATEGIC INVESTMENT AND IMPROVEMENTS FUND TO
22 ADVANCED TECHNOLOGY GRANT FUND. The office of management and budget shall
23 transfer $5,000,000 from the strategic investment and improvements fund to the advanced
24 technology grant fund during the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027.
25 SECTION 3. APPROPRIATION - DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE - ADVANCED
26 TECHNOLOGY GRANT PROGRAM - ONE-TIME FUNDING. There is appropriated out of any
27 moneys in the advanced technology grant fund in the state treasury, not otherwise appropriated,
28 the sum of $5,000,000, or so much of the sum as may be necessary, to the department of
29 commerce for the purpose of the advanced technology grant program, for the biennium
30 beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30, 2027. This funding is considered a one-time
31 funding item.
