7 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 15-10-12.1 of the North Dakota Century Code is
8 amended and reenacted as follows:
9 15-10-12.1. Acceptance of buildings and campus improvements - Legislative
10 approval.
11 The state board of higher education may authorize campus improvements and building
12 maintenance on land under the control of the board which are financed by donations, gifts,
13 grants, and bequests if the cost of the improvement or building maintenance is not more than
14 seven hundred thousand dollars. The consent of the legislative assembly is required for
15 construction of any building or any addition to a building on land under the control of the board
16 which is financed by donations, gifts, grants, and bequests. The consent of the legislative
17 assembly is required for campus improvements or building maintenance financed by donations,
18 gifts, grants, and bequests if the cost of the improvements or maintenance is more than seven
19 hundred thousand dollars. During the time the legislative assembly is not in session, except for
20 the sixthree months preceding the convening of a regular session and the three months
21 following the close of a regular session, and unless otherwise restricted by previous legislative
22 action or other law, the budget section may authorize the use of land under the control of the
23 board and construct buildings financed by donations, gifts, grants, and bequests and campus
24 improvements and building maintenance financed by donations, gifts, grants, and bequests if

2 project request submitted to the budget section for the construction of a building financed by
3 donations, gifts, grants, and bequests must include the removal of a building or buildings with
4 an equivalent or greater total number of square feet compared to the proposed building. The
5 budget section approval must comply with section 54-35-02.9 and must include a specific dollar
6 limit for each building, campus improvement project, or maintenance project. The state board of
7 higher education may authorize the sale of any real property or buildings which an institution of
8 higher learning has received by gift or bequest. The board shall prescribe such conditions for
9 the sale of the property as it determines necessary. The conditions must include requiring an
10 appraisal and public auction or advertisement for bids, unless the gift instrument requires a
11 different process. If the state board of higher education submits a request for campus
12 improvements, building maintenance, or to construct buildings under this section to the budget
13 section for approval, the legislative council shall notify each member of the legislative assembly
14 of the date of the budget section meeting at which the request will be considered and provide a
15 copy of the meeting agenda to each member of the legislative assembly. The chairman of the
16 budget section shall allow any member of the legislative assembly an opportunity to present
17 testimony to the budget section regarding any such request.
18 SECTION 2. AMENDMENT. Section 48-01.2-25 of the North Dakota Century Code is
19 amended and reenacted as follows:
20 48-01.2-25. Authorization of expansion of public improvements by legislative
21 assembly or budget section.
22 Notwithstanding any other provision of law, a state agency or institution may not
23 significantly change or expand a public improvement beyond what has been approved by the
24 legislative assembly unless the legislative assembly approves the change or expansion of the
25 project or any additional expenditure for the project. During the time the legislative assembly is
26 not in session, and unless otherwise restricted by previous legislative action or other law, the
27 budget section may approve a change or expansion or any additional expenditure for the
28 project. However, the budget section may not approve a change, expansion, or additional
29 expenditure for the project during the sixthree months preceding the convening of a regular
30 session or during the three months following the close of a regular session except for changes
31 in project scope and related additional expenditures resulting from an unforeseen emergency

3 addition to a building, including skywalks or other type of enclosed walkway, or any other
4 substantial increase in the area of the building, but does not include the construction of building
5 entrances and stairwells.

7 amended and reenacted as follows:
8 54-16-03. Unlawful to expend more than appropriated - May secure approval from

11 1. A state officer may not expend, or agree or contract to expend, any amount in excess

13 appropriated for any specific purpose or fund or for any other purpose without prior
14 approval in the form of a transfer approval or expenditure authorization as provided in
15 this chapter.

17 emergency commission an itemized petition requesting approval to:
18 a. Transfer money and spending authority between funds or line items pursuant to
19 section 54-16-04;
20 b. Accept and expendspecial funds or federal funds pursuant to section 54-16-04.1;

23 d. Accept and expend other funds pursuant to section 54-16-04.2; or
24 e. Recommend full-time equivalent positions pursuant to section 54-16-04.3.
25 3. The secretary of the emergency commission shall provide a copy of each petition
26 submitted under this section to the office of management and budget and legislative
27 council. The office of management and budget may provide an analysis and
28 prioritization of emergency requests to the emergency commission.
29 4. Any petition to the emergency commission by a state officer must be considered by
30 the emergency commission. A petition under this section must be approved or

3 5. Any debt or deficit created by a state officer in violation of this section is void.
4 SECTION 4. AMENDMENT. Section 54-16-04 of the North Dakota Century Code is
5 amended and reenacted as follows:
6 54-16-04. May order transfer of moneys between funds - Line item transfers - Order
7 may draw from state treasuryBudget section approval.
8 The emergency commission, upon the advice of the office of management and budget and
9 approval by the budget section of the legislative management, may order money or spending
10 authority transferred from one fund or line item to another fund or line item belonging to or
11 appropriated for the same institution or board or the same state enterprise, may order a transfer
12 of spending authority from the state contingencies appropriation, or, in an extremity, may
13 authorize money to be drawn from the state treasury to meet the emergency until the legislative
14 assembly can make an appropriation available. The following transfers, if authorized by the
15 emergency commission, require the approval of the budget section of the legislative
16 management:
17 1. A transfer of moneys or spending authority which would eliminate or make impossible
18 the accomplishment of a program or objective funded by the legislative assembly.
19 2. A transfer exceeding fifty thousand dollars from one fund or line item to another fund
20 or line item, unless the transfer is necessary to comply with a court order or to avoid:
21 a. An imminent threat to the safety of people or property due to a natural disaster or
22 war crisis; or
23 b. An imminent financial loss to the state. Any request considered by the budget
24 section must comply with section 54 - 35 - 02.9. The budget section, when
25 considering requests under this section, may approve the request in the form
26 received from the emergency commission or may amend and approve the
27 request. A request amended and approved by the budget section is deemed to
28 be approved by the emergency commission. Approval by the budget section is
29 not required if the transfer is necessary to avoid an imminent threat to the safety
30 of people or property due to a natural disaster or war crisis or an imminent
31 financial loss to the state.

5 amended and reenacted as follows:
6 54-16-13. Authority to borrow funds for a disaster - Continuing appropriation.
7 Any board, agency, commission, or officer of the state, subject to the approval of the
8 emergency commission and the budget section of the legislative management, may borrow
9 moneys from the Bank of North Dakota for the purpose of responding to a disaster within the
10 state as declared by the governor pursuant to chapter 37-17.1. The amount of moneys
11 borrowed is limited to the amount of any estimated federal reimbursement for repair, recovery,
12 or response relating to a disaster within the state, unless the state contingencies appropriation
13 is inadequate to pay the estimated state share of the costs, then moneys may be borrowed up
14 to one hundred percent of the costs incurred by the agency. Any interest payments on the funds
15 borrowed must be paid from funds availabletransferred to the agency from the state
16 contingencies appropriation as authorized by the emergency commission or other funds as
17 authorized by the emergency commissionand budget section. Any moneys borrowed from the
18 Bank of North Dakota pursuant to this section are hereby appropriated and may be spent by the
19 board, agency, commission, or officer of this state for the repair, recovery, or response relating
20 to a disaster within the state. If it appears to the borrower that at the end of the biennium the
21 amount available to repay the amount borrowed plus interest is insufficient to totally repay the
22 Bank of North Dakota, the borrower shall request from the legislative assembly a deficiency
23 appropriation sufficient for the repayment of the amount borrowed plus interest.
