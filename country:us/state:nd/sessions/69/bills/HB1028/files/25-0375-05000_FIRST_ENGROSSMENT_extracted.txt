1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact subsection 1 of section 48-01.2-20, subdivision f of
2 subsection 5 of section 48-01.2-20, subsection 6 of section 48-01.2-20, section 48-01.2-22, and
3 subsection 5 of section 54-44.7-03 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to the selection
4 process for construction management at-risk planning and design phase services and the
5 procurement of architect, engineer, construction management, and land surveying services.
6 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
7 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Subsection 1 of section 48-01.2-20 of the North Dakota
8 Century Code is amended and reenacted as follows:
9 1. A governing body electing to utilize a construction management at-risk delivery
10 process for a proposed public improvement shall create a selection committee
11 composed of:
12 a. An administrative individual from the governing body.
13 b. A registered architect, excluding any architect contracted for the project.
14 c. A registered engineer, excluding any engineer contracted for the project.
15 d. A licensed contractor.
16 SECTION 2. AMENDMENT. Subdivision f of subsection 5 of section 48-01.2-20 of the North
17 Dakota Century Code is amended and reenacted as follows:
18 f. The person's fees and expenses for the entire project, which must be identified
19 on a standard bid form approved by the director of the office of management and
20 budget and issued by the governing body for the project;
21 SECTION 3. AMENDMENT. Subsection 6 of section 48-01.2-20 of the North Dakota
22 Century Code is amended and reenacted as follows:

1 6. The selection committee shall evaluate each submission based on the qualification
2 criteria under subsection 5 without modifying the qualification criteria and shall include
3 the numeric scoring of each criteria item on a weighted basis, with. Excluding the
4 person's fees and expenses, which must be weighted no less than twenty percent, no

6 The weighting of the qualification criteria must be done in a manner to ensure no
7 subjective bias and encourage the maximum participation of qualified construction
8 managers at-risk. Each member of the selection committee shall apply the same
9 weighting of qualification criteria for a project.
10 SECTION 4. AMENDMENT. Section 48-01.2-22 of the North Dakota Century Code is
11 amended and reenacted as follows:
12 48-01.2-22. Subcontractor bids.
13 1. An agency construction manager selected for a public improvement shall publicly
14 advertise pursuant to section 48 - 01.2 - 04 and publicly open bids pursuant to section
15 48 - 01.2 - 07 from subcontractors for the work items necessary to complete the general
16 construction portions of the improvement. The agency construction manager shall
17 evaluate the bids and award the subcontract to the lowest responsible bidder. The
18 governing body may influence the selection of the subcontractors, but only insofar as
19 the governing body's past experience with a subcontractor or a current legal dispute
20 with a subcontractor.
21 2. A construction manager at-risk selected for a public improvement shall publicly
22 advertise pursuant to section 48 - 01.2 - 04 and publicly open bids pursuant to section
23 48 - 01.2 - 07 from subcontractors for the work items the construction manager at-risk

26 necessary to complete the general construction portions of the improvement the
27 construction manager a t - risk chooses to perform pursuant to this section . The
28 construction manager at-risk then shall evaluate the bids and determine which is the
29 most responsibleaward the subcontract to the lowest responsible bidder. The
30 governing body may influence the selection of the subcontractors, but only insofar as

2 with a subcontractor.
3 SECTION 5. AMENDMENT. Subsection 5 of section 54-44.7-03 of the North Dakota
4 Century Code is amended and reenacted as follows:
5 5. a. The agency selection committee shall evaluate each of the persons or firms
6 interviewed on the basis of the following criteria:
7 a. (1) Past performance.
8 b. (2) The ability of professional personnel.
9 c. (3) Willingness to meet time and budget requirements.
10 d. (4) Location, with higher priority given to firms headquartered in North Dakota.
11 e. (5) Recent, current, and projected workloads of the persons or firms.
12 f. (6) Related experience on similar projects.
13 g. (7) Recent and current work for the agency.
14 (8) Fees and expenses for the entire project, which must be identified on a
15 standard information submission form approved by the director of the office
16 of management and budget and issued by the agency selection committee
17 for the project.
18 b. This subdivision does not apply to:
19 (1) Section 24-02-07.3, unless the project is on the state capitol grounds and
20 exclusively involves state funds; or
21 (2) A project involving federal funds when the use of such funds prohibit this
22 criteria.
23 c. Based upon these evaluations, the agency selection committee shall select the
24 three which, in its judgment, are most qualified, ranking the three in priority order.
25 The agency selection committee's report ranking the interviewed persons or firms
26 must be in writing and must include data substantiating its determinations. This
27 data must be available to the public upon written request.
