Section 1:
25.1193.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
of North Dakota
FIRST ENGROSSMENT
Introduced by
Representatives Osowski, Hauck, Heilman, Maki, Morton
1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact section 15.1-07-34 of the North Dakota Century Code,
2 relating to training for mandated reporters of suspected child abuse or neglect; and to repeal
3 section 15.1-19-29 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to training for mandated
4 reporters of suspected child abuse or neglect.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
6 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 15.1-07-34 of the North Dakota Century Code is
7 amended and reenacted as follows:
8 15.1-07-34. Youth behavioral health and child abuse and neglect training tofor
9 teachers, administrators, and ancillary staff.
10 1. Every two years, eacha school district may provide a minimum of eight hours of
11 professional development on youth behavioral health, child abuse, and child neglect to
12 elementary, middle, and high school teachers, and administrators. EachA school
13 district may encourage ancillary and support staff to participate in the professional
14 development. Based on the annual needs assessment of the school district, these
15 hours may be designated from the following categories:
16 a. Trauma;
17 b. Social and emotional learning, including resiliency;
18 c. Suicide prevention;
19 d. Bullying;
20 e. Understanding of the prevalence and impact of youth behavioral health wellness
21 on family structure, education, juvenile services, law enforcement, and health
22 care and treatment providers;
Page No. 1 25.1193.02000
Sixty-ninth
Legislative Assembly
1 f. Knowledge of behavioral health symptoms, and risks;
2 g. Awareness of referral sources and evidence-based strategies for appropriate
3 interventions;
4 h. Other evidence-based strategies to reduce risk factors for students; or
5 i. Current or new evidence-based behavior prevention or mitigation techniques.
6 2. Each school within a district shall designate an individual as a behavioral health
7 resource coordinator.
8 3. The superintendent of public instruction shall collaborate with regional education
9 associations to disseminate information, training and instructional materials, and
10 notice of training opportunities to school districts and nonpublic schools. ThisThe
11 training must qualify for continuing education credits that count toward license
12 renewal.
13 4. The superintendent of public instruction shall maintain the contact information of the
14 behavioral health resource coordinator in each school.
15 5. Before initial licensure , a public or nonpublic school teacher, administrator, librarian ,
16 and counselor shall submit documentation to the education standards and practices
17 board verifying completion of a mandated reporter training program for suspected child
18 abuse or neglect. The department of health and human services shall develop and
19 provide an online interactive training module that may be used by a college of teacher
20 education, an individual educator, and an educational institution to fulfill the training
21 requirement. A college, school, and regional education association may offer a
22 mandated reporter training program that satisfy the requirements outlined in this
23 section for subsequent trainings at the district level.
24 SECTION 2. REPEAL. Section 15.1-19-29 of the North Dakota Century Code is repealed.
