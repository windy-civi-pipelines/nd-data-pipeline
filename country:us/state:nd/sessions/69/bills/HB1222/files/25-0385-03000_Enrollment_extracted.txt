Sixty-ninth Legislative Assembly of North Dakota
HOUSE BILL NO. 1222
(Representatives Novak, D. Anderson, Frelich, Hagert, Heinert, Porter, M. Ruby, Lefor)
(Senators Hogue, Luick, Meyer)
AN ACT to amend and reenact sections 15.1-09-33 and 15.1-19-03.1 of the North Dakota Century
Code, relating to a requirement for public school students to have the opportunity to recite the
pledge of allegiance each morning and immunity for liability stemming from the recitation of the
pledge of allegiance.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:

Section 2:
SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Section 15.1-09-33 of the North Dakota Century Code is amended and
reenacted as follows:

Section 3:
15.1-09-33. School board - Powers.
The board of a school district may:

Section 4:
1. Establish a system of free public schools for all children of legal school age residing within the
district.

Section 5:
2. Organize, establish, operate, and maintain elementary, middle, and high schools.

Section 6:
3. Have custody and control of all school district property and, in the case of the board of
education of the city of Fargo, have custody and control of all public school property within the
boundaries of the Fargo public school district and to manage and control all school matters.

Section 7:
4. Acquire real property and construct school buildings and other facilities.

Section 8:
5. Relocate or discontinue schools and liquidate the assets of the district as required by law;
provided no site may be acquired or building constructed, or no school may be organized,
established, operated, maintained, discontinued, or changed in location without the approval
of the state board of public school education if outside the boundary of the district.

Section 9:
6. Purchase, sell, exchange, and improve real property.

Section 10:
7. Lease real property for a maximum of one year except in the case of:
a. A career and technical education facility constructed in whole or in part with financing
acquired under chapter 40-57, which may be leased for up to twenty years; or
b. A lease for the installation of a wireless telecommunications facility, which may be leased
for up to twenty years.

Section 11:
8. Subject to chapter 32-15, exercise the power of eminent domain to acquire real property for
school purposes.

Section 12:
9. Purchase, sell, exchange, improve, and lease for up to one year equipment, furniture,
supplies, and textbooks.

Section 13:
10. Recruit or contract with others to recruit homes and facilities which provide boarding care for
special education students.

Section 14:
11. Provide dormitories for the boarding care of special education students.

Section 15:
12. Insure school district property.

Section 16:
13. Independently or jointly with other school districts, purchase telecommunications equipment or
lease a telecommunications system or network.

Section 17:
14. Provide for the education of students by another school district.

Section 18:
15. Contract with federal officials for the education of students in a federal school.

Section 19:
16. Prescribe courses of study in addition to those prescribed by the superintendent of public
instruction or by law.

Section 20:
17. Adopt rules regarding the instruction of students, including their admission, transfer,
organization, grading, and government.

Section 21:
18. Join the North Dakota high school activities association and pay membership fees.

Section 22:
19. Adopt alternative curricula for high school seniors who require fewer than four academic units.

Section 23:
20. Contract with, employ, and compensate school district personnel.

Section 24:
21. Contract with and provide reimbursement for the provision of teaching services by an
individual certified as an instructor in the areas of North Dakota American Indian languages
and culture by the education standards and practices board.

Section 25:
22. a. Suspend school district personnel;
b. Delegate to the superintendent of the district the authority to suspend school district
personnel, except as provided for in section 15.1-15-10; or
c. In the case of a district that does not employ a superintendent, delegate to the individual
charged with administering the district the authority to suspend school district personnel,
except as provided for in section 15.1-15-10.

Section 26:
23. Dismiss school district personnel.

Section 27:
24. Participate in group insurance plans and pay all or part of the insurance premiums.

26. Contract for the services of a principal.

person to perform the duties assigned to a school district business manager by law.

chapter 57-15.

Section 35:
32. Amend and certify budgets and tax levies, as provided in title 57.

Section 36:
33. Pay dues allowing for the board to hold membership in city, county, state, and national
organizations and associations.

of the district.

Section 38:
35. Authorize schools within the district to display the ten commandments with a display of other

Section 39:
36. Authorize schools within the district to permit students to recite the pledge of allegiance.

SECTION 2. AMENDMENT. Section 15.1-19-03.1 of the North Dakota Century Code is amended
and reenacted as follows:

Section 41:
15.1-19-03.1. Recitation of prayer - Period of silence - Pledge of allegiance.

Section 42:
1. A student may voluntarily may pray aloud or participate in religious speech at any time before,
during, or after the schoolday to the same extent a student may voluntarily may speak or
participate in secular speech.

Section 43:
2. A student of a public or nonpublic school may not be prohibited from voluntarily participating in
any student-initiated prayer at an activity held on the premises of a public or nonpublic school.

Section 44:
3. A schoolThe board of a school district, school administrator, or teacher may not impose any
restriction on the time, place, manner, or location of any student-initiated religious speech or
prayer which exceeds the restriction imposed on students' secular speech.

Section 45:
4. A schoolThe board mayof a school, by resolution, may allow a classroom teacher to impose
up to one minute of silence for meditation, reflection, or prayer at the beginning of each
schoolday.

Section 46:
5. A schoolThe board may authorize the voluntary recitation of a school distric t shall adopt a
policy requiring each school to offer all students the opportunity to voluntarily recite the pledge
of allegiance by a teacher or one or more students at the beginning of each schoolday. A
student may not be required to recite the pledge of allegiance, stand during the recitation of
the pledge of allegiance, or salute the American flag.
